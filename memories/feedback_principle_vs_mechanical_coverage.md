When a pattern repeatedly survives multiple validator passes uncorrected, the operative rule IS incomplete — even if the principle (criterion 1, §1 generative principle, atomic-thought test) is theoretically sufficient. The rule is incomplete because no mechanical signature translates principle into corpus-wide enforcement.

**Why:** Stan called this out 2026-05-09 on the Alma 30:18 compound-DObj case ("you say it's sufficient and yet it did not fire on several previous mechanical validator passes?"). I had said criterion 1 + N=2 was sufficient for compound-coordinate fragmentation; he correctly pointed out that if the validators kept missing it, the rule's operational coverage is what's actually broken. Principle-level adequacy and operational-coverage are different things.

**How to apply:** When I'm about to say "criterion 1 covers it" or "no new rule needed" or "the principle handles this" for a case the corpus has fragmented, run the follow-up check FIRST: (a) is there a mechanical UD signature for this pattern? (b) does an existing validator encode it? (c) if not, is the pattern codifiable as a new validator? If the corpus has the pattern in unswept form despite multiple validator runs, the operative answer is "rule is incomplete; needs a mechanical signature." Apply this discipline before declaring any case principle-covered.
