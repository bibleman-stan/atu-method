"Rhetorical force" ≠ "separate thought-line unit." A line that sounds powerful on its own (e.g., polysyndetic triple "to a wild and a hardened and a ferocious people") is NOT justified as a split just because it has impact — if it's syntactically the indirect object of a prior verb, it's not its own thought. Merge.

**Why:** Stan said explicitly: "I am not interested in rhetorical force per se, unless we are saying that it creates a separate thought-line unit." The test is always criterion 1.

**How to apply:** When evaluating whether a Rule 20 violation earns an "unless" exception, ask "does this line carry its own thought?" not "does this line sound powerful?" The camera-angle test (criterion 2) is valid — "from house to house" / "from place to place" genuinely repositions the mind's eye. But aesthetic force without thought-independence is not a justification.
