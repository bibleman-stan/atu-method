Punctuation in the v2-mine source is canonical LDS text — added by post-1830 editors, NOT original to the dictation. It must be preserved verbatim, but it has zero evidentiary weight for line-break adjudication.

**Why:** Stan called this out 2026-05-09 in a hostile correction ("WHAT IS OUR CLEAR RULE ABOUT USING PUNCTUATION FOR ANY SORT OF ADJUDICATION") after I justified an Alma 30:18 split with "em-dash signals a strong break-point." That logic treats inherited editorial overlay as if it were structural evidence. The 1830 BoFM was effectively unpunctuated — using punctuation to decide breaks would mean deferring to Victorian-era editors, not to the underlying grammar.

**How to apply:** When proposing or justifying a break/merge, the reasoning chain must rest on grammatical/syntactic facts (coordinate participials, complement integrity, compound DObj, frame+matrix one-proposition, M3/M4, Rule 17, etc.) — never on "the comma is here" or "there's an em-dash." Punctuation may *coincide* with a grammatically-justified break, but it is never the reason. If I catch myself writing "X signals a break" where X is a punctuation mark, retract and reframe on syntactic grounds. This applies equally to merge proposals — "no comma between" is not evidence for merge.
