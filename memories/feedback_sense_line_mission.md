**Three propositions, in priority order, that govern every colometric decision in the BOM Reader project:**

1. **Atomic thoughts trump rhetorical considerations.** A line that fails the atomic-thought test is wrong, regardless of how beautifully it preserves a Hebrew bicolon, an English parallelism, a chiastic structure, or any rhetorical figure. The four-criteria hierarchy (atomic thought > image > breath > source-language syntax) collapses to "atomic thought wins" whenever criteria collide.

2. **Our effort is to expose SENSE-lines, not poetic parallels.** The editorial mission is to surface discrete units of thought that an ESL reader, child, or newcomer can process one bite at a time. Hebrew/Greek poetic structure is incidental to that goal. When the two align, that's a happy bonus. When they conflict, atomic thought wins.

3. **We may overlap with Parry, but we are not "doing Parry."** Donald Parry's parallelism analysis already exists as the optional Hebrew Poetry layer in the build (loaded as the Parry index, 6604 verses). That layer surfaces poetic structure for readers who want it. The sense-line layer is something else: a delivery substrate for atomic thoughts. The two layers may overlap by ~78% but the overlap is incidental, not the design intent. Do not feel compelled to preserve a Parry parallel at the cost of an atomic-thought violation.

**Why:** Stan course-corrected an Isaiah block audit that had initially preserved Hebrew bicolon and tricolon structures across multiple lines (the "stacked parallels" approach following the old Rule 13). The audit had treated half-cola — hanging subjects, dangling relative clauses, verb-without-complement fragments — as acceptable because they were "preserving Hebrew structure." Stan's correction: "Parallels are fine, but we were trying to isolate clear ideas, not half strophes or poetic structure, except indirectly." Then in follow-up: "Atomic thoughts trump rhetorical considerations. Our effort here is to expose SENSE-lines, not poetic parallels. While we might overlap with Parry, we're not trying to 'do Parry.'" The famous failure case was 2 Ne 19:2 (Isaiah 9:2, "The people that walked in darkness / have seen a great light") rendered as 4 lines for 2 Hebrew cola, with each colon split at the relative-clause seam.

**How to apply:**

- When auditing or proposing colometric changes, run the atomic-thought test first and let it veto everything else. A line is either an atomic thought (or atomic image, or atomic breath unit) or it isn't. If it isn't, the line is wrong even if it produces a gorgeous rhetorical mirror.
- When you encounter a stacked parallelism (Isaiah-style bicola/tricola, Psalm-style parallelism, prophetic oracle structure), check whether each stacked element is *independently* atomic. If yes, stacking is good. If no, merge until each line stands as a complete thought — the parallelism may still be visible across the merged lines, but even if it isn't, atomic thought wins.
- Rule 13 ("parallel structures stack vertically") was amended on 2026-04-12 to: "stack vertically only when each stacked element is itself independently atomic." Documented in handoffs/10-colometry.md update dated 2026-04-12.
- When discussing Parry alignment, frame it as a *diagnostic* (Parry break-points can flag sense-lines worth examining), not a *target* (we never aim to reproduce Parry's cola). The Parry index lives in the Hebrew Poetry layer for readers who want it; the sense-line layer is the BOM Reader's primary delivery vehicle and serves a different purpose.
- This prioritization supersedes any earlier doc passage that suggested Hebrew structure should "take precedence" or that parallels should be preserved when in tension with atomicity. If you find such language in older handoffs, treat it as superseded.
