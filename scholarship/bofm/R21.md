# R21: Participial Absolute Integrity — Scholarship Companion

**Operational entry:** see `readers-bofm/private/01-method/colometry-canon.md §5 Rule 21` (current) or the proposed restructured entry at [`_drafts/R21-operational.md`](_drafts/R21-operational.md).

**Status:** This is the scholarship companion documenting WHY R21 exists, HOW we know it is correctly framed, and what intellectual and empirical history grounds its current shape. The operational canon entry says WHAT the rule does; this document says why.

---

## Statement

R21 (Participial Absolute Integrity) requires that a subject-bearing participial clause of the form *"X having Y-ed"* or *"X being Y"* occupy its own v2-mine line, distinct from its matrix predication. The participial absolute is a grammatically independent predication — its own subject + participle + optional complement — and is NOT a dangling modifier of the matrix.

## Rationale

The participial absolute is the canonical case where a non-finite construction nonetheless carries the weight of a full proposition. It supplies its own subject morphologically (*I, Nephi,...* / *I being...*), satisfies its own valence (participle + complement), and does so without depending on the matrix clause for syntactic completion. The reader can process the absolute as a self-contained cognitive frame — "this character had done / was in this state" — before the matrix predication describes what they then did.

Merging the participial absolute with its matrix would fragment two distinct frames: the backgrounded state-or-prior-event the absolute names, and the foregrounded matrix action. The atomic-thought test, applied at the proposition level, mandates own-line treatment because the absolute IS its own proposition under the finite-paraphrase diagnostic.

This is one of the framework's "non-predicated unit that functions as atomic thought via formal-structural recoverability" cases — except here the unit IS predicated, just non-finitely. R21 is therefore not a Structural Justification (J1–J5) extension of the generative principle; it is a direct application of the generative principle (§1.1: *each proposition splits by default*) to a participial proposition whose subject and predication are both morphologically present.

The interaction with framework §1.5 M3 (bare-governor indivisibility) is structural rather than precedential: M3 covers BARE participial heads without their own subject (subject-inheriting from matrix); R21 covers SUBJECT-BEARING participial absolutes. The two rules partition the participial space.

## Grammatical grounding

The participial absolute is a venerable construction in English with deep classical roots:

- **CGEL Chapter 14 §3** on absolute clauses and free adjuncts: the construction is described as a non-finite clause syntactically peripheral to the matrix, semantically backgrounded (state, prior event, attendant circumstance). CGEL distinguishes the absolute (with overt subject) from the bare participial adjunct (subject-controlled by matrix) — the same distinction that partitions R21 from M3.
- **Quirk et al., *A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language* §15.58–15.62** on absolute constructions: the absolute is described as a "supplementive clause" with its own subject, typically separated from the matrix by comma in writing and by intonational boundary in speech.
- **Latin grammar tradition** on the *ablative absolute* (Smyth §2070; Allen & Greenough §419): the closest classical analogue — a participial phrase with its own subject in an oblique case, semantically backgrounded relative to the matrix. The KJV-derived register of BoFM-English preserves this construction calqued into nominative-subject + comma-marked-frame English.
- **Greek genitive absolute** (Smyth §2070; BDF §423): analogous classical-language construction; the Greek New Testament makes heavy use of this pattern, and the KJV's English absolute constructions often translate Greek genitive absolutes directly.

The UD pattern for the construction is well-established: an `nsubj`-bearing non-finite VERB in participial form (`VerbForm=Part` or `VerbForm=Ger`) attached as `advcl`, `acl`, or `parataxis` to the matrix. The absence of a finite auxiliary (`aux:fin` or finite-VERB head) is the discriminator that separates the absolute from coordinated finite predication.

## Empirical evidence

### Canonical cases

The BoFM corpus contains numerous participial absolutes; the two canonical anchors are:

- **1 Nephi 1:1** *"I, Nephi, having been born of goodly parents, therefore I was taught somewhat in all the learning of my father..."* — the absolute *"I, Nephi, having been born of goodly parents"* sits on its own line; the matrix *"therefore I was taught..."* restarts on the following line. This is the corpus's opening verse and the most-cited participial absolute in the BoFM.
- **Mosiah 9:3** *"And yet, I being over-zealous to inherit the land of our fathers, collected as many as were desirous to go up to possess the land..."* — the absolute *"I being over-zealous to inherit the land of our fathers"* sits on its own line; the matrix *"collected..."* takes the absolute's subject as a restart subject on the following line.

Both cases pass the finite-paraphrase diagnostic cleanly: *"I, Nephi, had been born of goodly parents"* and *"I was over-zealous to inherit the land of our fathers"* are standalone propositions.

### Distinction from bare participials (M3 territory)

The BoFM corpus also contains a much larger population of bare participial heads — subject-inheriting participials that lack their own morphological subject. These are M3's territory, not R21's:

- *"telling them that there could be no atonement..."* — *telling* has no own subject; subject is inherited from the matrix.
- *"And it came to pass that I, Nephi, took my brethren, and went up into the wilderness, slaying the wild beasts..."* — *slaying* inherits subject from matrix.
- The 199 bare-trailing-participial corpus instances swept under the M3 extension (commit `25fb531`, 2026-05-08) are M3-class, not R21-class.

The R21-class population is a small minority — formal subject-bearing absolutes are a marked rhetorical construction in BoFM, used especially in autobiographical or first-person retrospective frames (Nephi's opening, Enos's prayer-narrative opening, Mosiah/Mormon editorial colophons).

### Diagnostic reliability

The finite-paraphrase test is highly reliable on the corpus. Of the canonical participial-absolute cases inspected, all pass the paraphrase (*"X had Y-ed"* / *"X was Y"*) test as a standalone proposition. Cases that fail the paraphrase test are typically bare participials (M3 territory) or participle-as-adjective constructions (e.g., *"the men, fleeing, were..."* where *fleeing* is attributive, not absolute).

## Intellectual lineage

R21 derives from the broader **proposition-grounding principle** in colometric editing: a clause-equivalent unit with its own subject and predication earns visual independence from the matrix. This principle is older than the apparatus and appears in:

- **Codex Vaticanus (B/03, ~325 CE) and Codex Sinaiticus** per-cola layouts of the Greek NT: Greek genitive absolutes consistently occupy their own cola, distinct from the surrounding finite-clause predication. The Pauline epistles and Lukan narrative provide abundant examples.
- **Classical Latin manuscript traditions** of Cicero, Caesar, and Tacitus: ablative-absolute constructions are typographically and prosodically separated from their matrix clauses in the editio-critica traditions.
- **The 1611 KJV's prose rhythm**: English absolute constructions — *"the day being far spent,"* *"these things being so,"* *"Jesus knowing all things that should come upon him,"* — are typographically set off by comma and frequently by line-break in the KJV's poetic-prose layout.
- **CGEL Chapter 14 §3 and Quirk et al. §15.58–15.62** as the modern grammatical formalization of the construction.

The apparatus's R21 instantiates this universal principle for the BoFM-English register, with the corpus's specific construction-inventory (first-person retrospective absolutes, editorial-colophon absolutes, prayer-narrative absolutes) drawn from BoFM's particular literary patterns.

The sibling readers-gnt canon's treatment of Greek genitive absolutes and the readers-tanakh canon's treatment of Hebrew nominal-absolute (extraposed) constructions are sister instantiations of the same universal absolute-as-proposition principle. The corpus-specific morphological inventories differ; the underlying absolute-earns-own-line treatment does not.

## Adversarial history

### 1. Initial codification

R21 was codified early in the canon to formalize the 1 Nephi 1:1 line-treatment, which had been established editorially before the rule was named. The naming made explicit what the corpus already practiced: subject-bearing participial absolutes earn their own line.

### 2. M3 partition (2026-05-08)

The M3 bare-trailing-participial extension (commit `fc02c78` codification; `25fb531` 199-instance sweep) raised the question of R21 × M3 boundary. Adversarial review concluded that the two rules partition the participial space cleanly by SCOPE — not by tier precedence:

- **R21:** participial with its OWN morphological subject (the absolute proper).
- **M3:** participial WITHOUT its own subject (subject-inheriting from matrix).

The partition is a SCOPE distinction rather than a precedence conflict. The two rules cannot fire on the same construction — by construction, a participial either has its own subject (R21 territory) or does not (M3 territory). The framework §1.5 M3 entry's "contrast with participial absolute" paragraph captures this partition explicitly: *"A full participial absolute with its own subject + participle + optional complement IS a complete predication and earns its own line (a structural-justification case, language-specific). M3 catches BARE participial heads WITHOUT the subject-bearing absolute structure."*

### 3. Action-code gap (migration-batch)

During the MISRA-style operational-template migration (this batch), the §Action-Codes table at [`docs/rule-template.md`](../../docs/rule-template.md) was found to lack a code for "the matched span occupies its own line, with breaks both before and after." The closest existing codes — `STACK_LIST_MEMBERS` (each member of a parallel series earns own line; plural-series oriented) and `KEEP_WHOLE` (no internal break; doesn't mandate boundary breaks) — are conceptual neighbors but neither expresses the unified own-line effect of R21.

Proposed extension: `STAND_OWN_LINE` — the matched span MUST occupy its own line, with breaks both before the span's start and after the span's end. R21 is the prototypical use. Other rules that may benefit from the same code: J2 portrait-accumulation single-portrait cases, J3 speech-act-announcement-line cases, J5 substantive-adjunct own-line cases.

Per §Action-Codes "new action codes require a meta-template change (§7.3 trigger #9)," the proposed `STAND_OWN_LINE` extension is flagged for migration-batch ratification rather than added unilaterally. Pending ratification, the rule's operational effect can be expressed as the conjunction of two existing `SPLIT_BEFORE_SUBJECT` operations (before the participial's subject and before the matrix's resumptive subject) — though the unified single code is preferred for clarity.

## Future work

### Validator implementation

R21 currently has no dedicated validator. Implementation requires:

- UD-feature pattern matcher: `nsubj`-bearing VERB in `VerbForm=Part` / `VerbForm=Ger` with no `aux:fin` dependent.
- Matrix-coupling check: the matrix clause must have its own subject (or co-referent restart) and its own finite predication.
- Negative-filter: exclude bare participials (no `nsubj` dependent) — those route to M3's validator.

Estimated corpus population: ~20–40 R21 instances based on spot-checks of opening verses and editorial colophons across the 15 BoFM books. The exact count awaits the validator's first corpus sweep (§7.3 trigger #12-a — post-codification goal-fit audit).

### Cross-corpus consistency

GNT's Rule on Greek genitive absolutes and Tanakh's Rule on Hebrew nominal-absolute (extraposed) constructions are sister instantiations of the same universal absolute-as-proposition principle. As the apparatus extends to additional corpora (Vulgate, Quran, Pali Canon, Sanskrit Veda), the absolute-integrity rule's instantiation pattern stabilizes:

- Identify the target language's absolute-construction morphology (genitive-absolute in Greek, ablative-absolute in Latin, nominal-absolute in Hebrew, locative-absolute in Sanskrit).
- Identify the target language's discriminator between absolute-with-own-subject and bare-participial-subject-inherited.
- Apply the same own-line mandate to the subject-bearing absolute; route the bare participial to the corresponding M3-equivalent.

This is the universal-principle-with-per-language-instantiation pattern that lets the apparatus generalize without copying rules.

### Vocative + participial absolute interaction edge cases

When a vocative opens a clause whose body is a participial absolute (*"O Lord, thou having delivered me..."*), R15 (vocative-own-line) and R21 (absolute-own-line) coexist: the vocative earns its own line, the absolute earns its own line, the matrix follows on a third line. The corpus contains few such cases; a future audit could enumerate them and verify the three-line treatment is consistent.

### Action-code ratification

The `STAND_OWN_LINE` action-code extension proposed above (§Adversarial history #3) should be ratified at migration-batch close. Other own-line-effect rules across the migration (J3 speech-act-announcement, J5 substantive-adjunct, J2 portrait-accumulation) would benefit from the same code; ratifying it once across the batch is cleaner than per-rule workarounds.
