# R11: Never End a Line on an Article — Scholarship Companion

**Operational entry:** see `readers-bofm/private/01-method/colometry-canon.md §5 R11`.

---

## Statement

R11 forbids any v2-mine line from ending on a determiner (`DET`). The DET must lead the next line with its head NP.

## Rationale

A determiner is obligatorily attributive — it projects a head noun (or noun phrase) on its right. Stranding a determiner at line end fragments the noun phrase across the line boundary and creates an open expectation that fails the atomic-thought test.

Generic English grammar, not a BoFM-specific editorial choice.

## Grammatical grounding

CGEL Chapter 5 §4-7 on the noun phrase: determiners are obligatory pre-head modifiers in articulated NPs. Quirk et al. (1985, §5.2-5.18) on determiner syntax. The line-final-DET case is the canonical example of NP-fragmentation.

UD's `DET` POS tag covers articles, demonstratives, possessive determiners, and quantifying determiners — all of which share the obligatory pre-head projection property.

## Empirical evidence

Layer-1 universality: applies to virtually all English texts. BoFM corpus compliance is ~100% post-application of `validate_line_final_tokens.py`.

## Intellectual lineage

Generic English grammar. Migration to Layer 1 happened during the layer-architecture restructuring, parallel to R9.

Sister Layer-1 rules in GNT canon: article + noun bond (Greek `ho`-class articles project right-attached nouns). Sister Layer-1 rules in Tanakh canon: definite-article `ha-` proclitic must precede its noun on the same prosodic word.

## Adversarial history

Long-settled rule. No substantive audit cycles. Same Layer 3 → Layer 1 migration as R9.

## Future work

None substantive. R11 is a floor-rule with universal English-grammatical grounding.
