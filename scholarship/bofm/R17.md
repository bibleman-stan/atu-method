# R17: Complement Integrity — Scholarship Companion

**Operational entry:** see `readers-bofm/private/01-method/colometry-canon.md §5 R17` (current) or this PoC's proposed restructured entry at [`R17-poc-operational.md`](R17-poc-operational.md).

**Status:** This is the scholarship companion documenting WHY R17 exists, HOW we know it's correctly framed, and what intellectual / empirical history grounds its current shape. The operational canon entry says WHAT the rule does; this document says why.

---

## Statement

R17 (Complement Integrity) requires that a matrix verb's clausal complement remain on the same line as the matrix verb when the verb's lemma belongs to one of six closed verb classes (Causative, Aspectual, Speech-indirect, Cognition, Volition, FEF-extraposition) and the complement is marked by *that*, *whether*, *if*, a WH-word, or an infinitival *to*.

## Rationale

The matrix verb's predication is grammatically incomplete without its complement. A line ending on *he caused* or *I say unto you* dangles forward — the reader's attention is left waiting for "caused what?" / "say what?" — and the dangling clause-head fails the atomic-thought test.

This isn't a matter of preference. The complement is an *obligatory argument* of the matrix verb's syntactic frame. Cognition verbs (*know*, *believe*, *perceive*) require an object of cognition; speech verbs (*say*, *declare*, *testify*) require an object of speech; causatives (*cause*, *suffer*, *command*) require an object of causation. Without the complement, the matrix verb is not predicating anything complete.

The apparatus's atomic-thought test, applied at the matrix-clause level, therefore mandates merge when the complement and matrix are on different lines. The rule is one of the framework's "syntax forbids splits" cases (`framework.md §1.2.2`): the matrix's valence is unsatisfied without its clausal complement, and a split that fragments the matrix from its complement violates the syntactic floor.

## Grammatical grounding

CGEL Chapter 14 §2 on coordination of verb phrases under shared auxiliary, and the broader CGEL treatment of clausal complementation (Chapter 11 on complements vs. modifiers; Chapter 14 on subordinate clauses). The six verb classes named in R17's closed list correspond to CGEL's classification of complement-taking verb types:

- **Causative** — CGEL terms these "implicative" or "causal" verbs; obligatorily take a clausal or to-infinitival complement specifying the caused event.
- **Aspectual** — CGEL Chapter 11 §6.2: verbs of inception (*begin*), continuation (*continue*), termination (*cease*) require an infinitival or gerundive complement specifying the aspectual scope.
- **Speech (indirect)** — CGEL Chapter 11 §10.3: indirect-discourse complements as obligatory arguments of speech-class matrix verbs. Distinguished from direct-discourse, which is a separate construction.
- **Cognition** — CGEL Chapter 11 §10.4: propositional-attitude verbs taking *that*-clause complements specifying the attitude's content.
- **Volition** — CGEL Chapter 11 §10.5: desiderative verbs taking *that*-clause or infinitival complements.
- **FEF-extraposition** — CGEL Chapter 16 on extraposition: "it is expedient that X" / "it was their lot to have fallen" patterns where a clausal complement is extraposed from a matrix predicate.

The Topic-PP extension (speech-class verbs requiring obligatory *of*/*concerning*/*unto*/*against* complement) is also CGEL territory (Chapter 6 on argument structure of speech verbs in archaic English registers; BoFM-archaic register preserves the early-modern-English pattern where these speech verbs take topic-PP as obligatory rather than adjunct).

The Experience *of*-PP extension (*repent of*, *partake of*, *forgive of*) is CGEL Chapter 6 on the early-modern-English pattern of experiential verbs requiring an *of*-PP specifying the content of the experience.

## Empirical evidence

### Phase-1 hostile audit (2026-04-23)

Corpus-wide sweep on R17 violations after initial codification. Findings:

- **Speech-class verb + obligatory topic-PP:** ~265 merged corpus instances (compliant); ~10 split corpus instances (R17-violating). The 10 violations cluster in relative-clause environments where the long subject-NP induces line-break pressure.
- **`speak`/`spake`/`spoken` + `of|concerning|unto|against`:** 265:10 merged:split ratio.
- **`testify` + topic-PP:** 46 merged.
- **`bear record|testimony|witness` + topic-PP:** 12 merged.
- **`repent of`/`partake of` (Experience *of*-PP extension):** 68 merged. 1 pre-audit split (Alma 24:10 *"hath forgiven us of those our many sins"*) resolved by audit.

Corpus consistency is ~96% merge. The ~10 violations cluster in a single relative-clause-environment shape — the canonical violation site.

### Phase-2 audit (2026-05-10 Wave 6)

Closed-list-promotion audit on the Rule 17 directive-petition sub-clause. Findings:

- **Detector dead-code defect (Defect A):** `DIRECTIVE_PETITION = {cry, pray, beseech, ask, seek, plead}` contained `{beseech, ask, plead}` which were NOT in `GOVERNING_LEMMAS`. So 14 corpus cases (5 beseech + 1 ask + 8 plead) were silently unrouted. Fixed in detector commit `9f0e09f`.
- **Prose mis-attribution (Defect B):** Initial canon-promotion draft said "subset of speech-class verbs" but `pray` and `seek` are VOLITION-class. Two of three corpus-active members were not speech-class. Corrected to "subset of speech- and volition-class verbs naming petitional/directive speech acts."
- **Bandwagon-shape label (Defect C):** Initial label "directive-petition" was speech-act-theory terminology (Searle/Austin shape). Renamed to "petition-frame ambiguity filter" — describes the mechanical trigger conjunction (verb-set × modal-aux) rather than ontologic class. Detector variable renamed `DIRECTIVE_PETITION` → `PETITION_FRAME_VERBS`.

### Speech-indirect long-complement exception

7 corpus instances, all currently split, all protected by R17's long-complement exception:

- 1 Ne 15:27 *"said unto them / that the water...was filthiness"*
- 1 Ne 15:29 *"said unto them / that it was a representation of that awful hell..."*
- 1 Ne 15:32 *"said unto them / that it was a representation of things both temporal and spiritual"*
- 1 Ne 16:2 *"said unto them / that I knew that I had spoken hard things..."*
- 1 Ne 16:25 *"said unto them / that they should murmur no more..."*
- Alma 9:31 *"said unto them / that they were a hard-hearted and a stiffnecked people"*
- Alma 9:32 *"said unto them / that they were a lost and a fallen people"*

All 7 have complement bodies ≥8 words and short speech-tags (no participial scene-setting frame). Editorial practice across all 7 supports the exception.

### Petition-frame ambiguity (2026-05-10 Wave 6)

14 corpus cases routed to REVIEW under the petition-frame ambiguity filter. The construction *"I cried unto God that he would preserve the records"* is genuinely ambiguous between:

- Content reading (UD `ccomp`): the prayer's content was "may God preserve the records"
- Purpose reading (UD `advcl`): the cry was for the purpose of God preserving the records

LLM parsers tag this as `ccomp`, but the semantic ambiguity warrants editorial review rather than mechanical merge. Empirically: of the 14 routed cases, ~50% read more naturally as purpose-clauses (warranting Rule 7 split) and ~50% as content-clauses (warranting R17 merge). The 50/50 distribution is itself evidence that mechanical resolution is unsafe.

## Intellectual lineage

R17 derives from the broader **complement-integrity principle** in scholarly editing: a matrix verb and its obligatory clausal complement form one cognitive frame and should be presented as one visual unit. This principle is older than the apparatus; it appears (in different formal vocabulary) in:

- **TEI Guidelines** P5 §16 on linguistic-corpus annotation of complement structure.
- **CGEL** Ch. 11 §10 on indirect discourse and propositional-attitude complements.
- **Macula-Greek's clause-boundary annotations** (Clear Bible's lowfat XML), where ccomp clauses are annotated as constituents of the matrix clause rather than as independent units.
- **Codex Vaticanus (B/03, ~325 CE) and Codex Sinaiticus** per-cola layouts of the Greek NT: verbatim historical precedent for keeping matrix speech-verbs + indirect-discourse complements on one cola.

The apparatus's R17 instantiates this universal principle for the BoFM-English register, with the six verb classes calibrated to the corpus's specific lexical inventory. The sibling readers-gnt canon's Rule 8 (Complementizer *hoti* — cognition vs. speech) and readers-tanakh canon's Rule H7 (Complement integrity for clausal complements of *amar*-class verbs) are sister instantiations of the same universal principle.

The Topic-PP extension is specifically a BoFM-archaic-register phenomenon. Modern English speech verbs typically take a direct-object NP (*"he spoke truth"*); BoFM's KJV-derived speech-verb syntax uses PP-marked topic complements (*"he spake of truth"*). This is not a peculiarity of the BoFM but inherited from early-modern English usage preserved in the 1611 KJV translation tradition.

## Adversarial history

R17 has been subjected to four substantive audit cycles, each producing material refinements:

### 1. Initial codification (2026-03-12)

Six closed verb classes + the *that*/*whether*/*if*/WH/infinitival mark inventory. Adversarial audit raised:

- Risk: closed-list verb membership underspecified. Resolution: enumerate all corpus-attested members per class (~45 lemmas total); commit to lemma-list discipline.
- Risk: exceptions for direct discourse, AICTP-that, purpose-that not formalized. Resolution: add Exclusions list (initially 4 items; now 8 after subsequent extensions).

### 2. Topic-PP extension audit (2026-04-23)

Phase-1 hostile audit. Findings:

- Corpus consistency ~96% (265:10) for the topic-PP merge pattern across all speech-class verbs.
- 10 violations cluster in relative-clause-environment shape — single failure mode.
- Adjacent of-PP pattern for `repent`/`partake`/`forgive` is structurally identical (Experience *of*-PP); audit recommended folding into R17 rather than creating a sibling rule.

Outcome: R17 extended with Topic-PP and Experience-of-PP sub-rules. Corpus sweep applied 10 merges to bring violations to zero.

### 3. Parallel that-series doctrinal-weight retirement (2026-04-23 AM, retired 2026-04-23 PM)

A proposed sub-rule for N=2 coordinate *that*-series under cognition-class verbs initially included an enumerated list of "doctrinal-weight" cases (Pauline-calque, testimony-cadence, sacrament-prayer, covenant/ordinance, prophetic-rhythm). Adversarial audit retired this within hours of codification:

- Four of five enumerated categories failed the mechanical-identifiability test (could not be detected from syntax/morphology alone).
- The list shape-matched the rhetoric-bandwagon failure mode (see [`../../memories/feedback_rhetoric_bandwagon.md`](../../memories/feedback_rhetoric_bandwagon.md)) — ad-hoc curated named-list masquerading as a mechanical rule.

The substantive content of the rule (N=2 cognition-class clean-cognate cases) was preserved via the §1 N=2 Adjudication Principle plus M1 synonymy-test. The enumerated doctrinal-weight list was discarded entirely.

This audit is referenced in §1 N=2 Adjudication Principle's "default-to-B under synonymy-test uncertainty" clause, which restates §2's general discretion for the N=2 R17 context rather than re-introducing the failed enumeration.

### 4. Wave 6 closed-list promotion audit (2026-05-10)

Audited four closed-list canon promotions including the directive-petition sub-clause. Findings retold above. Material refinements:

- Defect A (detector dead-code) — 14 corpus cases unrouted, fixed.
- Defect B (prose mis-attribution) — speech-class label corrected to speech- and volition-class.
- Defect C (bandwagon-shape label) — "directive-petition" renamed to "petition-frame ambiguity filter."

The rename specifically targets the rhetoric-bandwagon failure mode: speech-act-theory terminology imports an ontologic frame (Searle/Austin) that isn't load-bearing for the mechanical trigger. The new name describes what the filter mechanically detects (verb-set × modal-aux conjunction) rather than what speech-act-theory would call the resulting class.

## Future work

### Cross-corpus consistency

GNT's R8 (Complementizer hoti) and Tanakh's H7 (Complement integrity) are sister instantiations of the same universal complement-integrity principle. As the apparatus extends to additional corpora (e.g., Vulgate, Quran), the complement-integrity rule's instantiation pattern stabilizes:

- Identify the target language's complement-taking verb classes (closed-list inventory).
- Identify the target language's complement-marking morphology (subordinator inventory, infinitival markers).
- Apply the same closed-list-mark-merge logic with the language's syntactic inventory.

This is the universal-principle-with-per-language-instantiation pattern that lets the apparatus generalize without copying rules.

### Phase 2 (discourse-context) refinement

The petition-frame ambiguity exception (~14 BoFM corpus cases) is REVIEW-tier because content-vs-purpose resolution requires discourse context the validator can't see. Phase 2 of the apparatus, if extended with discourse-entity tracking, would let petition-frame cases be mechanically resolved: when the matrix subject's prior discourse-context establishes a desire-frame, content-reading wins; when the prior context establishes an action-frame, purpose-reading wins. This is future infrastructure work; for now the REVIEW bucket is the honest output.

### Long-complement threshold tuning

The speech-indirect long-complement exception currently uses a hard threshold (≥8 word tokens). 7 corpus instances clear this threshold; some additional instances at the 5-7-word range have been hand-evaluated case-by-case. A future audit could tune the threshold (lower it slightly to capture more clean cases; calibrate against the 96% merge consistency baseline) or replace it with a graduated scoring function. For now the binary threshold suffices.
