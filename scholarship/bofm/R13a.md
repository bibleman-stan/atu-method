# R13a: Never End a Line on a Preposition Seeking Its Object — Scholarship Companion

**Operational entry:** see `readers-bofm/private/01-method/colometry-canon.md §5 R13a`.

---

## Statement

R13a forbids any v2-mine line from ending on an adposition (`ADP`) that has a pending `case` relation to an object on the following line. The ADP must lead the next line with its object.

## Rationale

A preposition that hasn't yet attached to its object is grammatically incomplete — the PP is fragmented across the line boundary. Stranding fails the atomic-thought test at the line boundary because the reader sees the case-marker without its case-marked content.

Generic English grammar with two well-known exceptions: phrasal-verb particles (which are part of the verb's lexical structure, not pending-case adpositions) and stranded prepositions in relative clauses (where the preposition's object is the relative pronoun, syntactically permitted to be discontinuous).

## Grammatical grounding

CGEL Chapter 7 §8-12 on prepositions and PP structure. Quirk et al. (1985, §9.1-9.62) on preposition syntax. The exclusions for phrasal-verb particles and stranded prepositions are themselves CGEL-recognized exceptions (CGEL Ch. 4 §16 on phrasal verbs; Ch. 12 §28 on preposition stranding).

UD distinguishes `ADP` (adposition) from particle/phrasal-verb-particle uses via the `compound:prt` relation, which is the formal signal for Exclusion 1. Stranded prepositions retain `ADP` POS but their case-relation points to an antecedent that has been raised out of the local PP — the formal signal for Exclusion 2.

## Empirical evidence

Layer-1 universality applies. The two exclusions (phrasal-verb particles, stranded prepositions in relatives) are corpus-attested in BoFM English (KJV-derivative register has preposition stranding as a regular feature). The validator at `validate_line_final_tokens.py` uses the UD parse to distinguish the genuine R13a violation from the licensed exceptions.

## Intellectual lineage

Generic English grammar. Migration to Layer 1 happened during the layer-architecture restructuring, parallel to R9 and R11.

Sister Layer-1 rules in GNT canon: preposition + object bond (Koine prepositions take obligatory object case — accusative, genitive, dative — and the PP cannot fragment). Sister Layer-1 rules in Tanakh canon: prefixed inseparable preposition (`b-`, `l-`, `k-`, `m-`) must remain attached to its host noun on the same prosodic word.

## Adversarial history

Long-settled rule. The exclusion list (phrasal-verb particles, stranded prepositions) was clarified during initial codification; no substantive subsequent audit cycles. Same Layer 3 → Layer 1 migration as R9 and R11.

The "13a" suffix preserves backward compatibility with an earlier numbering scheme that distinguished R13a (preposition + object) from a hypothetical R13b (postposition cases) — the latter never codified in English. Renumbering to plain R13 was considered and rejected to preserve commit-message and cross-reference stability.

## Future work

None substantive. R13a is a floor-rule with universal English-grammatical grounding, plus two well-defined exclusions that the UD parse handles cleanly.
