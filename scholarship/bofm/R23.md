# R23: Date Colophon Integrity — Scholarship Companion

**Operational entry:** see `readers-bofm/private/01-method/colometry-canon.md §5 Rule 23` (current).

**Status:** This is the scholarship companion documenting WHY R23 exists, HOW we know it is correctly framed, and what intellectual and empirical history grounds its current shape. The operational canon entry says WHAT the rule does; this document says why.

---

## Statement

R23 (Date Colophon Integrity) requires that a date-colophon formula — the BoFM's recurring narrative timestamp construction *"in the Nth year of the reign of the judges"* (and its closed-list variants *"in the Nth year of the reign of king <name>"* and *"in the Nth year since Lehi left Jerusalem"*) — be kept whole on a single line. The formula is treated as a single lexicalized unit; no line break is permitted internal to its token sequence. The own-line placement of the formula as a fronted or trailing temporal PP is licensed by J5 (substantive adjunct as own focus) and is a separate matter from R23's internal-indivisibility mandate.

## Rationale

Date colophons are the BoFM's bureaucratic time-stamping device. Each formula marks a fixed temporal anchor — a specific year in a specific reign — and operates as a single coherent unit of when-information. Splitting the formula internally produces no valid atomic-thought reading: *"in the forty and second year"* dangles forward, expecting *"of the reign of the judges"* to complete the timestamp; *"of the reign of the judges"* dangles backward, expecting the year-NP to anchor it. Neither half is a proposition, and neither half functions as a formally-marked atomic unit under any of the five structural justifications.

The formula's indivisibility is therefore a syntax-veto case under `framework.md §1.2.3` (formula integrity): a lexicalized multi-word frame that functions as a single unit. Any split the generative principle would otherwise produce internal to the formula is blocked by R23's KEEP_WHOLE mandate.

The own-line placement of the date-colophon as a fronted temporal PP is a separate matter handled by J5. The colophon is grammatically peripheral to the matrix predication's core truth (the events of the year, not the year itself, are the predication's subject and verb) and carries substantial content (the full reign-anchored timestamp). It reliably passes J5's slot-paraphrase test — *"in what time-frame did X happen?"* — and earns its own line as a substantive temporal adjunct. This own-line placement is the canonical surface shape for date-colophons in the BoFM corpus.

## Grammatical grounding

Syntactically, a date-colophon is a fully formed prepositional phrase with:

- **Preposition** *in* (UD: `case`) heading the temporal PP.
- **Determiner** *the* (UD: `det`) — required article on the ordinal-NP head.
- **Ordinal modifier** (UD: `amod`) — the year-number, optionally compound (*forty and second*).
- **Head noun** *year* (UD: head of the temporal PP).
- **Nested PP** *of the reign of the judges* / *of the reign of king <name>* / temporal *since* clause — a chain of `nmod` PP-modifiers specifying the reign-anchor.

The construction's archaic register — *of the reign of the judges* as a recurrent governmental-era marker — is documented in the Book of Mormon's distinctive Nephite chronology, which dates events by the reign-year of the Nephite judges (post-Mosiah, ~91 BCE onward) and earlier by reign-year of kings or by years-since-departure-from-Jerusalem. CGEL Chapter 4 §3 on temporal PP structure and Chapter 7 on appositive nmod-PPs treats the underlying grammatical pattern; the BoFM's specific lexicalization of this pattern as a fixed timestamp formula is a register-specific phenomenon documented in BoFM stylometric scholarship.

The Layer 3 status of R23 (rather than Layer 1) reflects the fact that the formula's indivisibility is an editorial recognition of the lexical-bundle status, not a generic English mid-phrase prohibition. A naïve syntactic parser could in principle accept a break inside *year / of the reign* — the `nmod` PP is grammatically separable — but the editorial discipline rejects it because the unit is lexicalized as a single timestamp marker. The reign-PP is not a free `nmod` adjunct; it is the obligatory anchor that distinguishes *the forty and second year* (of which reign?) from a complete date.

## Empirical evidence

### Corpus prevalence

Date-colophons are concentrated in the BoFM's narrative books — Mosiah, Alma, Helaman, 3 Nephi, Mormon, 4 Nephi, and Ether — where they timestamp the year-by-year chronology of the Nephite-Lamanite conflict and post-Christ Nephite history. Helaman in particular uses the formula as a chapter- and section-opening device, with date-colophons frequently fronted to a new line as the temporal anchor for the events that follow.

The *"in the Nth year of the reign of the judges"* variant is by far the most frequent (Mosiah 29 onward, throughout Alma, Helaman, and 3 Nephi). The *"in the Nth year of the reign of king <name>"* variant appears in 1 Nephi (referencing Zedekiah's reign) and in the earlier Mosiah-era chronology. The *"in the Nth year since Lehi left Jerusalem"* variant appears in 1 Nephi and 2 Nephi as the pre-judges-era timestamp device.

### Compliance

The v2-mine corpus is uniformly compliant with R23 — every matched date-colophon token-sequence occurrence is whole on its line. The validators at `validators/colometry/validate_rule_23_date_colophon.py` (surface-pattern) and `validators/colometry/validate_rule_23_ud.py` (UD-query) report zero violations across the corpus. The UD validator additionally distinguishes STRONG-MERGE-CANDIDATE (gap ≤ 3 lines, genuine split) from REVIEW-REQUIRED (gap > 3 lines, likely line-mapping drift rather than a true date-formula split) — the latter bucket is a precaution against false positives from sentence-anchor drift in the parser pipeline.

The compliance rate is unsurprising: date-colophon indivisibility was one of the early editorial settlements in the project's history and has been enforced from the first hand-edited v2 source files. The rule codifies a discipline that was already universal in practice, motivated by the fact that any reader scanning for chronology needs the full timestamp on one visual line to extract the year-reign anchor at a glance.

### Own-line interaction (J5)

The canonical surface shape for a date-colophon in the v2-mine corpus is its own-line treatment as a fronted temporal PP. Representative cases:

- Helaman 10:2 *"And now behold, it came to pass / in the commencement of the fortieth year of the reign of the judges over the people of Nephi, / [matrix predication]"* — AICTP (R1) on line 1, date-colophon (R23, J5-licensed own line) on line 2.
- Helaman 10:288 *"And now it came to pass / in the forty and third year of the reign of the judges, / there was no contention among the people of Nephi"* — same shape.
- Alma 9-era *"in the seventh year of the reign of the judges"* — comparable fronted-temporal treatment.

The own-line treatment is J5's domain. R23 cares only that the colophon's internal tokens stay whole regardless of which line they occupy. When the colophon trails the matrix (closing-bracket usage, e.g., Helaman 10:411 *"in the latter end of the forty and eighth year of the reign of the judges over the people of Nephi"*), the same logic holds — R23 internal indivisibility, J5 license for own-line trailing placement.

### Compound-ordinal handling

The BoFM's distinctive *"forty and second"* / *"twenty and seventh"* compound-ordinal pattern (number-word + *and* + ordinal) is preserved as part of the formula's lexicalized form. The UD validator (`validate_rule_23_ud.py` lines 99-117) explicitly allows the compound-ordinal sub-pattern inside the formula's anchor span. The compound form is not editorially modernized to *"forty-second"* — punctuation and word-content are inherited from the source per the universal preservation principle (`framework.md §1`).

## Intellectual lineage

R23 derives from the broader **formula-integrity principle** in scholarly editing: a lexicalized multi-word frame that functions as a single discourse marker should be presented as one visual unit. R23 is one of three sibling formula-integrity rules in the BoFM canon — alongside R1 (AICTP formula) and R18 (fixed idiom list) — each governing its own closed-list span under the same KEEP_WHOLE logic.

The date-colophon is a culturally distinctive feature of the BoFM. The Nephite reckoning system (year-of-reign anchored to the judges, kings, or Lehi-departure) is a chronological device specific to this corpus. Comparable formula-integrity targets in sibling canons:

- **GNT** has no direct analog; Greek narrative chronology relies on different formulae (e.g., the *"in the fifteenth year of the reign of Tiberius Caesar"* construction in Luke 3:1 is a one-off, not a recurring formula warranting its own rule).
- **Tanakh** has reign-year formulae (*"in the Nth year of <king>"*) throughout Kings and Chronicles. A sister rule in the Tanakh canon (to be specified during readers-tanakh canon migration) is the appropriate sibling instantiation of the same universal principle (`framework.md §1.2.3`).
- **Ancient codex tradition** — Codex Vaticanus (B/03) and Codex Sinaiticus per-cola layouts of the Greek OT historical books treat reign-year formulae as single-cola units, confirming the keep-whole convention has deep textual-tradition precedent.

The Layer 3 placement of R23 (rather than absorbing it into Layer 1) is principled: Layer 1 codifies generic English syntactic prohibitions; R23 codifies the editorial recognition that the BoFM's date-colophon is a register-specific lexical bundle. A different English text could legitimately break *year / of the reign of the judges* if the reign-PP were a free modifier; in the BoFM, the reign-anchor is obligatory to the timestamp's identity, and the editorial discipline treats the full span as one unit.

## Adversarial history

R23 has not been subjected to substantive adversarial audit cycles because the rule's content is uncontroversial: the date-colophon is universally recognized as a fixed lexical-bundle timestamp in the BoFM editorial tradition, and corpus compliance has been complete from the first v2-mine edition. The rule was codified in the early tranche of canon rules and has not required refinement.

Two adjacent disputes were considered and resolved:

1. **Should R23 be a separate rule or a sub-clause of R18 (fixed idiom)?** Resolved in favor of separation. R18's fixed-idiom closed list contains short fully-lexicalized expressions (*put to death*, *from time to time*); date-colophons are templated — variable in their ordinal slot — and the closed list is parameterized rather than literal. The detection logic is also distinct: R18 matches surface strings; R23 matches a templated spine with ordinal/number-word slots. Separating them keeps each rule's validator simple. R18's current §5 entry's *Fixed-idiom list* nonetheless cross-references R23 ("Date-colophon formulas (Rule 23)") to acknowledge the sibling-rule relationship.

2. **Should the own-line treatment be part of R23 or J5?** Resolved in favor of J5. R23 governs only formula-internal indivisibility; the own-line placement of the formula as a fronted/trailing temporal PP is J5's domain (substantive adjunct as own focus). This separation lets R23 stay a pure KEEP_WHOLE rule and lets J5 govern the broader class of substantive temporal-PP own-line treatments uniformly (which includes non-date temporal PPs that pass J5's slot-paraphrase test for other reasons). The separation also avoids inflating R23's scope into territory it does not properly own.

No retirement, narrowing, or expansion of R23 has been seriously proposed. The rule's exceptions list is small and consists of clean cross-references to sibling rules (R1, R16, R18, J5).

## Future work

### Variant inventory completeness

The DATE_COLOPHONS_R23 closed list currently enumerates three formula templates: *of the reign of the judges*, *of the reign of king <name>*, and *since Lehi left Jerusalem*. A future audit could verify the inventory is exhaustive — that no fourth template (e.g., a *since the coming of Christ* variant in 3 Nephi / 4 Nephi, or an *of the reign of <name>* variant for non-king Nephite leaders) appears in the corpus with the same lexicalized status. The UD validator (`validate_rule_23_ud.py`) already detects the anchor pattern *in the Nth year* and could be extended to flag any matching anchor occurrence whose tail does not match the closed list, ensuring no novel variant slips by without explicit registration.

### Coupling with chapter/book colophons

The current rule covers year-formulas only. The BoFM also contains book- and chapter-level colophons (*"The book of Mosiah,"* *"Behold I make an end of speaking concerning these things..."*) which are structurally different — they are independent metanarrative editorial frames rather than within-verse temporal PPs. A future extension could codify colophon-integrity rules for these editorial-frame cases, parallel to R23 but distinct in their UD signatures (the metanarrative colophons are full clauses, not PPs). Defer until book-/chapter-level formatting work is in scope.

### Cross-corpus consistency

As the apparatus extends to the readers-tanakh corpus, the Hebrew reign-year formulae (*bishnat <ordinal> le-<king-name>*) are the direct sister instantiation of R23. The same KEEP_WHOLE logic applies, with the closed list parameterized on Hebrew reign-name inventory rather than English. Coordinating R23 with the Tanakh sister rule (when codified) is the natural next step in the apparatus's cross-corpus formula-integrity coverage.

### REVIEW-REQUIRED bucket sizing

The UD validator's REVIEW-REQUIRED bucket (gap > 3 lines) was sized as a precaution against line-mapping drift. A future audit of the empirical REVIEW-REQUIRED bucket (zero hits at the time of writing) could either tighten the threshold (gap > 2) or eliminate the bucket entirely if line-mapping infrastructure is verified stable across the corpus. The conservative threshold cost nothing in the current sweep (no false positives or false negatives observed) but adds detector complexity.
