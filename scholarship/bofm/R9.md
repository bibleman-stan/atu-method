# R9: Never End a Line on a Conjunction — Scholarship Companion

**Operational entry:** see `readers-bofm/private/01-method/colometry-canon.md §5 R9`.

---

## Statement

R9 forbids any v2-mine line from ending on a coordinating conjunction (`CCONJ`). The conjunction must lead the next line.

## Rationale

A coordinating conjunction (`and`, `or`, `nor`, `but`, `for`, `yet`, `so`) is grammatically a forward-pointer — it announces a following member that completes the coordination. Stranding it at line end creates an open expectation that fails the atomic-thought test: the reader's attention is left waiting for what comes next, and no line ending mid-coordination can be a complete unit of meaning.

This is generic English grammar, not a BoFM-specific editorial choice. Any competent editor of any English text would observe the same prohibition.

## Grammatical grounding

CGEL Chapter 14 on coordination: coordinating conjunctions are obligatorily two-sided — they project a left conjunct AND a right conjunct. Quirk et al. (1985, §13.20-13.26) on coordinator syntax. The line-final-CCONJ case is the canonical example of a violated grammatical projection.

UD's `CCONJ` POS tag is intentionally distinct from `SCONJ` (subordinating conjunctions) precisely because the bidirectional-projection property differs.

## Empirical evidence

Layer-1 universality: applies to virtually all English texts. BoFM corpus compliance is ~100% post-application of `validate_line_final_tokens.py`. The rule is a floor-violation detector, not a corpus-distribution metric.

## Intellectual lineage

This is generic English grammar codified in any modern reference grammar. Migration to Layer 1 (from an earlier nominal placement at Layer 3) happened during the layer-architecture restructuring; the migration was a recognition that R9 makes no BoFM-specific editorial claim — it is a syntactic floor any English text obeys.

Sister Layer-1 rules in GNT canon: line-initial Greek postpositives forbidden (similar projection-violation logic for the opposite-handed postpositive class). Sister Layer-1 rules in Tanakh canon: maqqef-bound proclitic-stranding forbidden.

## Adversarial history
