# R6: Causal Clauses Break — Scholarship Companion

**Operational entry:** see `readers-bofm/private/01-method/colometry-canon.md §5 R6` (current) or this migration's proposed restructured entry at [`_drafts/R6-operational.md`](_drafts/R6-operational.md).

**Status:** This is the scholarship companion documenting WHY R6 exists, HOW we know it's correctly framed, and what intellectual / empirical history grounds its current shape. The operational canon entry says WHAT the rule does; this document says why.

---

## Statement

R6 (Causal Clauses Break) requires that a finite causal clause — introduced by the subordinator *because* and attaching as `advcl` to a matrix VERB or ADJ — be split from that matrix. The break is inserted before the *because* mark. Non-clausal *because of* + NP prepositional constructions are out of scope. Fronted-*because* (causal clause preceding the matrix) is routed to REVIEW because the break direction differs from the trailing case. The rule yields to R17 when the *because*-clause sits inside a `ccomp` under a Rule-17 governor, where firing R6 would sever the R17 matrix from its *that*-complement.

## Rationale

A finite causal clause is an adverbial explanatory modifier — a new cognitive frame distinct from the action it explains. *"They did murmur against their father"* is one event with its own atomic frame; *"because he had brought them out of the land"* is a separate frame specifying the cause of that event. The reader processes the action first, then the explanation; running them together compresses two frames into one line in a way that fails the atomic-thought test for the cause-frame member.

The break is grammatical-structural rather than rhetorical: the *because*-clause has its own finite verbal nucleus (a finite verb under its embedded subject), its own subject (often co-referential with the matrix subject, but a fresh syntactic subject under the embedded predication), and its own complement structure. Each finite verbal nucleus is a candidate predication per the §1.1 generative principle; the *because* subordinator confirms the embedded clause's adverbial-causal function.

The *because of* PP construction behaves differently: it lacks a finite embedded clause, has no embedded subject or verb, and reads as a single integrated cause-modifier on the matrix predication. *"They were destroyed because of their wickedness"* is one event-with-cause composite; the prepositional *because of their wickedness* does not earn its own atomic frame. This is why the rule's scope excludes the PP construction.

## Grammatical grounding

The grammatical-structural distinction between finite causal clauses (*because + S + V*) and prepositional causal modifiers (*because of + NP*) is standard in English-grammatical reference works:

- **CGEL Chapter 11** on adverbial clauses, particularly the section on causal subordination: finite *because*-clauses are classified with other finite adverbial subordinate clauses (purpose, conditional, temporal) — each forming its own predicational frame.
- **CGEL Chapter 14 §1** on subordinate clauses: finite adverbial subordinators (*because*, *since*, *as*, *for*) take finite-clausal complements and form their own predicational nucleus.
- **CGEL Chapter 7** on prepositional phrases distinguishes *because of* + NP (PP construction) from *because* + finite clause (subordinator + S). The two constructions have different syntactic weight; the PP construction does not project an independent predication.
- **Smyth Greek Grammar §2240ff.** on Greek causal subordinators (*ὅτι*, *διότι*, *ἐπεί*, *ἐπειδή*) introducing finite causal clauses — the cross-language structural parallel. Causal subordination is consistently a finite-predication frame when it has its own finite verb.

R6 sits in the broader **finite-clausal-adverbial split** family with R7 (Purpose Clauses Break — *that* + MODAL) and the framework's §1.1 generative principle. The differentiation between R6 (causal) and R7 (purposive) is the mark inventory and the cognitive function (cause vs. telos), not a deeper structural difference. Both finite adverbial clauses earn their own atomic frame.

The fronted-*because* asymmetry has principled grammatical basis: trailing causal clauses present cause AFTER effect (the canonical English information-structure order — assertion-then-explanation), while fronted causal clauses present cause BEFORE effect (a marked information-structure choice — typically frame-setting or topic-establishment). The two require different break treatments because the cognitive sequencing differs. Mechanical resolution of the fronted case would require discourse-structural reasoning the validator can't perform reliably; REVIEW is the honest output.

## Empirical evidence

### Corpus realization

The *because*-subordinator pattern is well-attested across the BoFM corpus. Phase 2 sweep (2026-05-10) produced 40 STRONG-SPLIT applications after the validator added two filters:

1. **`because of NP` PP-construction filter** — `has_of_case` test on the *because* node; PP constructions skipped.
2. **`advcl_head.upos in {VERB, ADJ}` filter** — restricts firing to genuine finite-adverbial-causal attachments.

After filtering, 9 cases routed to REVIEW (8 fronted-*because* + 1 short-combined-line atomic-thought merge under M4). The fronted-*because* cluster is the principal REVIEW-tier residual; the *because of NP* construction is a clean mechanical out-of-scope.

### The Rule 17 precedence-guard discovery (Alma 41:10)

A pre-guard application of R6 at **Alma 41:10** — *"Do not suppose, because it has been spoken concerning restoration, that ye shall be restored..."* — produced a Rule 17 violation: the R6 split severed the matrix verb *suppose* from its *that*-complement (*"that ye shall be restored..."*) by inserting the *because*-clause as an own-line frame between them. The R6 detector had matched on the *because*-clause's `advcl` attachment without checking whether the *because*-clause was structurally embedded inside a `ccomp` under an R17-class governor.

The corrective discipline: R6's detector MUST traverse the parent chain of the *because*-advcl to verify no enclosing R17-governed `ccomp` before firing. When such an enclosing `ccomp` is present, R6 yields to R17 — the matrix governor's complement-integrity bond with its *that*-complement is a Tier 3 precedence claim that wins over R6's Tier 5 generative split.

This was discovered during the 2026-05-10 UD-detector framework / corpus-sweep session and recorded in the session's `pending.md` as a detector cross-rule precedence guard requiring codification. The guard is now reflected in R6's operational Exclusion 3 (this migration).

### Fronted-because: review-tier residual

Of the 9 REVIEW-tier cases after Phase 2 filtering, 8 are fronted-*because* constructions. The current REVIEW disposition is conservative — fronted-causal break direction differs from trailing-causal (the break belongs after the fronted clause, not before *because*), and mechanical resolution requires recognizing the fronted-then-matrix sequencing. Future detector work could codify a fronted-position diagnostic (e.g., line-initial *because* with subsequent finite-clause + comma + matrix predication) and apply break-after-fronted-clause; for now the REVIEW bucket is the honest output.

## Intellectual lineage

R6 derives from the broader **adverbial-clause split principle** in sense-line editorial practice: a matrix predication and its finite adverbial-clause modifier are two cognitive frames and should be presented as two visual units. This principle is older than the apparatus; it appears in:

- **Codex Vaticanus (B/03, ~325 CE) per-cola layouts** of the Greek NT: finite ὅτι/διότι causal clauses are routinely placed on their own colon, distinct from the matrix predication they explain. This is the historical precedent for causal-clause break.
- **Codex Sinaiticus** colometric arrangements follow the same pattern.
- **Modern scholarly editions** of biblical and classical texts using sense-line presentation typically break finite adverbial clauses from their matrix.

The apparatus's R6 instantiates this universal principle for the BoFM-English register, with the *because* subordinator as the corpus's principal lexical realization of finite-causal subordination. The sibling readers-gnt canon's equivalent rule (Greek finite ὅτι/διότι causal split) and readers-tanakh canon's equivalent rule (Hebrew finite *ki*/*ya'an* causal split) are sister instantiations of the same universal principle, each calibrated to their target language's finite-causal marking morphology.

R6 also sits in the cross-cutting **complement-integrity-vs-adjunct-split precedence pattern** with R17. The pattern recurs across the apparatus: split-trigger rules at Tier 5 (R6, R7, R19) consistently yield to complement-integrity rules at Tier 3 (R17, R26) when their respective triggers nest inside complement-integrity bonds. The R6 → R17 precedence is one instance of this universal precedence-claim shape.

## Adversarial history

R6 has been subjected to multiple audit cycles, each producing material refinements:

### 1. Initial codification

Finite *because*-clause split — simple `advcl` with `mark = because`, break before *because*. The rule was initially under-specified in three ways the corpus quickly exposed:

- No explicit *because of NP* PP-construction carve-out (the PP construction was ambiguously treated by the surface-pattern detector)
- No fronted-*because* handling (break direction differs)
- No R17 precedence guard (firing R6 inside an enclosing R17-governed ccomp produced complement-integrity violations)

### 2. PP-construction exclusion codification (Phase 2 sweep, 2026-05-10)

The validator added two filters during Phase 2:
- `has_of_case` test on the *because* node to detect *because of NP* PP constructions
- `advcl_head.upos in {VERB, ADJ}` to restrict firing to genuine finite-adverbial-causal attachments

After filtering, 40 STRONG-SPLIT applications were applied corpus-wide. Pre-filter, the surface pattern would have over-fired on the PP construction; the filtered detector now cleanly separates the clausal (R6 scope) and prepositional (out of scope) constructions.

### 3. Rule 17 precedence-guard discovery (Alma 41:10, 2026-05-10)

Applying the post-filter R6 sweep produced a Rule 17 violation at Alma 41:10 *"Do not suppose, because it has been spoken concerning restoration, that ye shall be restored..."*. The R6 split severed the matrix *suppose* from its *that*-complement. The detector had matched on the *because*-clause's `advcl` attachment without checking whether the entire construction was nested inside an R17-governed ccomp.

The corrective claim: R6's detector MUST traverse the parent chain of the *because*-advcl to verify no enclosing R17-governed ccomp before firing. When such an enclosing ccomp is present, R6 yields to R17. This is recorded as Exclusion 3 in the operational entry and reflected in the §3.5 Tier 5 precedence (R6 yields to R17 in nested complement-integrity contexts).

The discovery is a canonical instance of the cross-rule precedence pattern: a generative split-trigger (R6) nested inside a complement-integrity bond (R17) must yield to the integrity bond. The same precedence pattern applies to R7, R19, and other Tier 5 split-triggers when they nest inside Tier 3 complement-integrity rules.

### 4. Fronted-because routing to REVIEW (Phase 2, 2026-05-10)

Of the 9 cases left REVIEW-tier after Phase 2 filtering, 8 are fronted-*because* constructions. The decision was made to route fronted-*because* to REVIEW rather than auto-apply a different break direction, because:

- Fronted-then-trailing-matrix information-structure differs from trailing-causal
- The break belongs AFTER the fronted clause, not before *because* (the canonical R6 break direction)
- Mechanical resolution would require recognizing the fronted-vs-trailing position reliably

The fronted-position diagnostic is a future-work item; for now REVIEW is the honest output.

## Future work

### Cross-corpus consistency

The readers-gnt canon's Greek-finite-causal rule and readers-tanakh canon's Hebrew-finite-causal rule are sister instantiations of the same universal principle. As the apparatus extends to additional corpora (e.g., Vulgate *quia*/*quoniam* causal, Quran finite *li-* causal), the finite-causal-split rule's instantiation pattern stabilizes:

- Identify the target language's finite-causal marking morphology (subordinator inventory).
- Identify the target language's prepositional-causal construction(s) and codify them as out-of-scope.
- Apply the same advcl-attachment-with-finite-marking split logic with the language's syntactic inventory.

### Fronted-because mechanization

The 8 fronted-*because* cases currently routed to REVIEW could be mechanically resolved with a fronted-position diagnostic. A future audit could:

1. Codify a UD signature for fronted-causal (line-initial *because*-advcl preceding the matrix predication).
2. Apply break-after-fronted-clause (rather than break-before-*because*) as the canonical fronted-case action.
3. Sweep the 8 REVIEW cases under the new diagnostic and apply mechanically.

This is per-corpus mechanization work; the universal principle (finite-causal earns own frame regardless of position) is already established.

### Sister-subordinator coverage

The current `CAUSAL_MARK_LEMMAS` set is restricted to `because`. Other finite-causal subordinators (*since*, *as*, *for*, *seeing that*, *forasmuch as*, *inasmuch as*) appear in the BoFM-archaic register with varying frequency. A future audit could verify per-lemma corpus attestation and decide which to fold into R6 vs. keep as separate rules. The recurring discipline is that lemma additions to a closed list are §7.3 trigger #6 (mechanical-signature change under a settled rule) and require audit.

### Cross-rule precedence-guard generalization

The R6 → R17 precedence guard is one instance of a general pattern: generative split-trigger rules at Tier 5 should detect nested complement-integrity contexts at Tier 3 before firing. A future canon-meta refinement could codify a universal "Tier 5 nested in Tier 3 → yield" rule covering R6, R7, R19, and any future generative split-trigger. For now the precedence is hand-codified per-rule.
