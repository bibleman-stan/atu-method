# EP-5: Virtue/Vice Lists — Scholarship Companion

**Operational entry:** see `readers-bofm/private/01-method/colometry-canon.md §5 EP-5` (current).

**Status:** This is the scholarship companion documenting WHY EP-5 exists, HOW we know it is correctly framed, and what intellectual / empirical history grounds its current shape. The operational canon entry says WHAT the rule does; this document says why.

---

## Statement

EP-5 (Virtue/Vice Lists) directs editors confronting a coordinate stack of moral-quality NPs or ADJs — virtue lists or vice lists — to first examine the stack for a formally-marked rhetorical parallel pattern (dual, triadic, crescendo, antithetic pairing) before deciding the break. When a marked parallel pattern is detected and the stack qualifies under J1's formally-marked parallel series, each member earns its own line per J1 (Tier 5 stacking, confirmed by EP-5 within Tier 7). When no parallel pattern is detected, the stack defaults to merge as a compound-list complement of its governing predicate. Genuinely ambiguous cases route to REVIEW.

EP-5 is a Category B editorial principle (judgment-required) and a Tier 7 post-hoc tiebreaker — it fires only when Tiers 1-6 (syntax vetoes, formula/vocative integrity, complement integrity, merge-overrides, split-triggers, N=2 adjudication) have not already settled the break. At N≥3 with marked parallel, J1 settles the case before EP-5 is consulted; EP-5's role there is confirmatory.

## Rationale

Virtue and vice lists are a distinctive feature of the BoFM-archaic register — moral-quality NPs (faith, hope, charity, patience, long-suffering, mercy; or pride, envy, hatred, malice) stacked under a shared governing predicate (*"have faith, hope, and charity"*; *"full of patience, mercy, and long-suffering"*; *"hath neither faith, hope, nor charity"*). The stacks vary widely in their cognitive weight for the reader:

- **Compound-complement stacks** are *one cognitive event*. The matrix predicate scopes over the stack as a single complement; no member carries independent predicative force; the reader processes the stack as one chunk specifying the complement of the governing predicate. *"hath neither faith, hope, nor charity"* is one negation event over a triple complement, not three negation events.
- **Formally-marked parallel stacks** are *N cognitive events*. When formal markers (repeated possessive, repeated preposition, polysyndetic *and*, fixed liturgical ordering) are present, each member earns its own beat — the stacking IS the rhetorical structure. The fixed *faith / hope / charity* triad in didactic / sermonic contexts (Moroni 7:1, Moroni 10:20-21, Ether 12:28-37) is the canonical example.

EP-5 codifies the distinction so that line-break placement reveals it: stack the parallel; merge the non-parallel. The rule does NOT generate breaks beyond what J1's parallel-series formal markers already license; it only adjudicates whether a virtue/vice stack should be merged as a compound complement or stacked as a parallel series, in the residual decision space where Tiers 1-6 have not already settled the case.

The rule is editorial (Category B) because the parallel-pattern detection requires reading the stack for rhythmic / formal shape — dual, triadic, crescendo, antithetic — and these shapes depend on inter-member regularities the UD parse does not represent. The same triad (*faith / hope / charity*) can read formulaic-triadic in one context (own-line per member, J1-confirmed by EP-5) and compound-complement in another (single-line merge under the matrix predicate's scope). Per-case editorial judgment is therefore irreducible.

## Grammatical grounding

Stacked moral-quality NPs are a special case of the more general coordinate-noun-phrase construction in English. CGEL Chapter 15 §1 on coordination treats coordinate NPs as conjuncts of a single phrase headed by the coordinating conjunction; the shared governing predicate scopes over the entire coordinate structure. The default cognitive treatment is **compound complement** — one event with a coordinate object — until formal marking reveals **parallel beats** that each carry independent attention weight.

Quirk et al. *Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language* (CGEL-Quirk, 1985) §13.45-13.73 on coordination distinguishes between:

- **Mere coordination** — bare *and*/*or* between members of a compound complement; no inter-member predicative weight.
- **Polysyndetic coordination** — repeated *and* / repeated preposition / repeated possessive between members; each member carries its own beat. CGEL-Quirk §13.74 explicitly notes that polysyndetic coordination has rhetorical weight beyond the syntactic conjunction — each conjunction acts as an attentional reset.
- **Asyndetic coordination** — comma-only joining without explicit conjunction; ambiguous between mere coordination and polysyndetic shape; depends on member count and formal context.

The EP-5 diagnostic 1 (pattern-detection scan) operationalizes the polysyndetic-coordination test against the BoFM corpus's virtue/vice lemma inventory.

Classical rhetoric names the polysyndetic-coordination pattern as *polysyndeton* (Greek *polysyndeton*, "much-binding"); the asyndetic counterpart is *asyndeton* (Greek *asyndeton*, "unbound"). Both figures are catalogued in Quintilian's *Institutio Oratoria* IX.iii.50-54 and have been studied extensively in Pauline-corpus virtue/vice-list scholarship (see *Intellectual lineage* below). For the EP-5 apparatus, classical-rhetoric vocabulary is reference-only — the operational test rests on formal markers, not on classical-rhetoric category-imports (see *Adversarial history* below for the rhetoric-bandwagon discipline that constrains EP-5's framing).

## Empirical evidence

### Corpus distribution (informal, pre-systematic-sweep)

Initial corpus survey identified the following virtue/vice stack inventory in v2-mine:

- **Faith / hope / charity triad** — corpus-attested in Alma 7:24 (*"And see that ye have faith, hope, and charity"*), Moroni 7:1, Moroni 7:26-44, Moroni 10:20-21 (*"for he hath neither faith, hope, nor charity"*), Ether 12:28-37 (*"that faith, hope and charity bringeth unto me"*), Moroni 8:14 (*"which he spake concerning faith, hope, and charity"*). ~7-8 instances corpus-wide. The triad shows BOTH single-line (compound-complement) and stacked (J1-parallel) treatments depending on register: didactic / sermonic contexts favor stacked; negative-existential or AICTP-framed contexts favor single-line.
- **Patience / mercy / long-suffering triad** — corpus-attested in Alma 9:26 (*"full of patience, mercy, and long-suffering"*), Alma 7:23 (*"full of patience and long-suffering"* — N=2 variant), Alma 13:28. ~3-4 instances corpus-wide. Treatment mirrors faith/hope/charity (context-dependent).
- **Compound-divine-attribute stacks** — Mosiah 4:6 (*"and his matchless power, and his wisdom, and his patience"*), Alma 9:26 (*"full of grace, equity, and truth"*). ~5-10 instances. These mix attribute classes (power / wisdom / patience are not all virtues in the moral-quality sense); they sit on the EP-5 boundary and frequently route to REVIEW.
- **Vice-list stacks** — pride / envy / hatred / malice clusters in Mormon 8 / Moroni 9 vice catalogues; whoredom / iniquity / wickedness clusters in Helaman / Mormon judgment passages. ~10-15 instances. Vice-lists tend toward polysyndetic stacking when used rhetorically (catalogue effect) and toward single-line listing when used as compound complement.
- **N=2 virtue pairs** — *faith and hope*, *meek and lowly*, *chastity and virtue*, *faith and patience*. ~20-30 instances corpus-wide. N=2 routes to §1.9 N=2 Adjudication Principle (M1 synonymy test) before reaching EP-5; most resolve to M1 merge as cognate / bonded pairs.

A full-corpus systematic sweep with the current rule shape has not yet been run. Pending tasks for EP-5 corpus-fit work are tracked in BoFM canon §7.3 trigger #12 (post-codification corpus-fit verification) — EP-5 was carried forward through the 2026-04-19 restructuring without a fresh full-corpus sweep, so the sweep remains an open obligation if EP-5 is materially refined again.

### Faith / hope / charity contrast (canonical illustration)

The faith / hope / charity triad is the densest illustration of EP-5's bimodal treatment:

- **Stacked treatment (J1 + EP-5 confirms):** Alma 7:24 in didactic context (*"And see that ye have / faith, / hope, / and charity"*) — the imperative + repeated definite-article-less members in liturgical order produces clean parallel rhythm; each member earns its own beat per J1's polysyndetic-coordination signal.
- **Merged treatment (EP-5 default-merge):** Moroni 10:21 in negative-existential context (*"for he hath neither faith, hope, nor charity"*) — the *neither...nor* frame scopes over the triad as a single negation event; no member earns independent predicative weight; merge as compound complement.
- **AICTP-framed treatment (R1 wins before EP-5):** When the triad appears inside an AICTP formula span, R1 formula integrity wins at Tier 2 before EP-5 is consulted; the triad is preserved on the formula's line regardless of internal pattern.

This bimodal distribution is the empirical motivation for EP-5's "default-merge unless marked parallel detected" framing — the same lemma triad does not have a single correct treatment, and the disambiguation depends on the surrounding frame's rhetorical use.

### Tier 7 ordering precedent

EP-5's Tier-7 placement was confirmed during the 2026-05-10 UD-detector-framework session, alongside EP-1, EP-3, EP-4. The session formalized the §3.5 precedence hierarchy and placed all EP-rules at Tier 7 as post-hoc editorial tiebreakers. For EP-5 specifically, the Tier-7 placement has a particular interaction with J1: when a virtue/vice stack at N≥3 carries formal parallel markers, J1 (Tier 5) settles the case before EP-5 is consulted (per the §1.9 N=3+ cliff). EP-5's effective decision-space is therefore narrower than EP-1's — most clean parallel-marked cases are already settled by J1; EP-5 handles the residual where J1's formal markers are absent or weak and the stack must be classified as compound-complement vs. parallel by editorial judgment.

## Intellectual lineage

### Virtue/vice list scholarship in biblical studies

Virtue and vice lists (German *Tugend- und Lasterkataloge*) are a well-attested literary form in Greco-Roman and Second-Temple-Jewish literature. The NT instantiates the form prolifically (Galatians 5:19-23 fruit-of-the-Spirit list, 2 Peter 1:5-7 virtue ladder, 1 Corinthians 13 charity catalogue, Romans 1:29-31 vice list, Colossians 3:5-14 paired vice/virtue lists). The form has been studied across:

- **Anton Vögtle**, *Die Tugend- und Lasterkataloge im Neuen Testament* (1936) — the foundational German monograph cataloguing NT virtue/vice lists and their Hellenistic-Jewish parallels.
- **Siegfried Wibbing**, *Die Tugend- und Lasterkataloge im Neuen Testament und ihre Traditionsgeschichte unter besonderer Berücksichtigung der Qumran-Texte* (1959) — extends Vögtle with Qumran (1QS, 1QM, 4QInstruction) catalogue parallels.
- **Ehrhard Kamlah**, *Die Form der katalogischen Paränese im Neuen Testament* (1964) — formal-rhetorical analysis of NT paraenetic catalogues, distinguishing *Tugendkatalog* (virtue catalogue), *Lasterkatalog* (vice catalogue), and *Haustafel* (household code).
- **Hans Dieter Betz**, *Galatians* (Hermeneia, 1979) §5.19-23 commentary — treats Galatians 5:19-23 as a hybrid virtue/vice catalogue derived from Stoic *prokopē* (moral-progress) traditions.

The BoFM virtue/vice lists are downstream of this tradition — the *faith / hope / charity* triad is explicitly Pauline (1 Corinthians 13:13), and the paired virtue-stacks (*patience / mercy / long-suffering*; *meekness / lowliness*) follow NT-derived liturgical patterns. EP-5 instantiates the apparatus's treatment of this form for the BoFM-archaic register; the form itself is not a BoFM peculiarity but inherited from KJV-mediated NT literary conventions.

### Polysyndeton in classical rhetoric

The polysyndetic-coordination signal that EP-5 uses to detect parallel pattern has its own classical-rhetoric pedigree:

- **Quintilian** *Institutio Oratoria* IX.iii.50-54 — polysyndeton as figure of *amplificatio* (rhetorical amplification through multiplied conjunctions).
- **[Pseudo-]Demetrius** *On Style* §54 — polysyndeton as figure of *deinotes* (forcefulness).
- **Lausberg** *Handbook of Literary Rhetoric* §686-689 — modern systematic treatment.

The apparatus's J1 (formally-marked parallel series) directly operationalizes polysyndeton as a structural-justification trigger. EP-5's pattern-detection diagnostic uses polysyndetic signals (repeated possessive, repeated preposition, polysyndetic *and*) as parallel-pattern indicators, but it does NOT import classical-rhetoric ontology beyond the formal-marker recognition. See *Adversarial history — rhetoric-bandwagon discipline* below.

### Editorial-principle vs. mechanical-rule distinction

EP-5's status as Category B (editorial, judgment-required) reflects the same foundational distinction that grounds EP-1, EP-3, EP-4: rules whose disambiguation requires reading the construction against discourse / rhetorical context that the UD parse cannot represent. The parallel-pattern detection for virtue/vice stacks specifically requires:

- Inter-member uniformity (member-class consistency, member-length similarity, repeated formal markers).
- Rhetorical register sensitivity (didactic / sermonic register favors stacked; narrative / AICTP-framed register favors merged).
- Liturgical-formula recognition (*faith / hope / charity* triad as fixed liturgical unit; *patience / mercy / long-suffering* triad as fixed scriptural unit).

None of these features is mechanically extractable from the parse. EP-5 surfaces candidates and routes to editorial judgment, by design.

## Adversarial history

### 1. Initial codification (pre-2026-04-19, as part of original §5 rule set)

EP-5 was originally formulated as a rule within the §5 mechanical-rule sequence, alongside what later became EP-1, EP-3, EP-4. The initial framing was operational: stacked moral qualities may exhibit rhetorical parallel patterns (dual, triadic, crescendo); when a pattern is detected, line breaks should reveal it; when no pattern is detected, default to merge. Adversarial review at the time raised:

- **Risk: the rule could become a doctrinal-weight catch-all.** Early drafts considered enumerated subclasses of virtue lists with named exemptions (*sacrament-prayer virtues*, *baptismal-covenant virtues*, *charity hymn (1 Corinthians 13-style)*). Resolution: no enumerated subclasses; the rule operates on formal marker detection only. See *rhetoric-bandwagon discipline* below.
- **Risk: the rule could over-fire on N=2 cognate pairs.** Resolution: explicit interaction with §1.9 N=2 Adjudication Principle — N=2 pairs route to M1 synonymy test before EP-5 is consulted; EP-5 fires only on N≥3 stacks or on N=2 cases that M1 leaves unresolved.

### 2. Reclassification to EP-tier (2026-04-19)

When the BoFM canon was restructured into mechanical-rules vs. editorial-principles tiers (2026-04-19 three-layer architecture session — see `private/2026-04-19-canon-v2-and-merge-overrides/transcript.md`), the original rule was reclassified as EP-5 alongside the three other judgment-required rules (EP-1 according-to manner vs. source, EP-3 inverted predicate, EP-4 title/role + domain).

The reclassification recognized that the parallel-pattern detection, while well-grounded in classical-rhetoric polysyndeton and in NT-tradition virtue/vice catalogue scholarship, cannot be reduced to a mechanical UD test. The UD parse identifies the coordinate structure but does not represent the inter-member formal-uniformity signals (member-length, repeated-marker, register-sensitivity) that determine whether the stack is a compound complement or a parallel series. The disambiguation is therefore irreducibly editorial.

### 3. Rhetoric-bandwagon discipline (cross-cutting)

EP-5's framing is constrained by the rhetoric-bandwagon discipline documented in [`../../memories/feedback_rhetoric_bandwagon.md`](../../memories/feedback_rhetoric_bandwagon.md). The discipline resists wholesale adoption of classical-rhetoric or Hebrew-parallelism category-imports as rule-justifying ontology. Specifically for EP-5:

- The rule does NOT import a closed list of "named virtue subclasses" (Pauline virtues vs. Stoic virtues vs. monastic virtues) as gating categories. Such an enumerated subclassification was considered in an early draft and retired as bandwagon-shape — ad-hoc named subclasses masquerading as a mechanical rule. The same failure mode retired the R17 doctrinal-weight enumerated list (2026-04-23 AM codified, retired same day) and the participial-merge editorial carve-outs (M3 extension, 2026-05-05).
- The rule uses **polysyndetic-coordination formal markers** (repeated possessive, repeated preposition, polysyndetic *and*) as parallel-pattern indicators. These ARE mechanical signals — they can be detected from the surface form. The classical-rhetoric vocabulary (*polysyndeton*) is reference-only; the operational test rests on the formal-marker presence, not on the classical-rhetoric category-membership.
- The rule does NOT make doctrinal-weight claims about which virtue lists are "more important" or "more canonical." All virtue/vice stacks pass through the same diagnostic; rhetorical-register sensitivity emerges from the formal markers, not from doctrinal pre-classification.

This discipline is the conceptual basis for EP-5's narrow operational scope: detect formal parallel markers, route to J1 if present, default to merge if absent, route to REVIEW if ambiguous. No category-import beyond that.

### 4. Pending: post-codification corpus-fit verification

The pre-2026-04-19 codification rested on an informal corpus survey across the high-virtue-density books (Alma 7, Alma 13, Moroni 7-10, Ether 12). Per BoFM canon §7.3 trigger #12 (post-codification corpus-fit verification), a full-corpus systematic sweep remains an open obligation. The sweep would:

- Enumerate every coordinate stack of moral-quality NPs/ADJs in v2-mine (likely ~50-80 instances across the corpus, given the formulaic frequency of *faith / hope / charity* and vice-catalogues).
- Apply the pattern-detection diagnostic under the current rule shape.
- Verify line-break treatment matches the rule's direction for unambiguous cases (parallel-marked → J1 stacked; unmarked → merged).
- Enumerate residual ambiguous cases for editorial review (likely the mixed-attribute stacks at the EP-5 boundary).

Until this sweep is run, EP-5's claim of corpus-wide conformity rests on partial-sample evidence.

## Future work

### Phase-2 discourse-context refinement

The parallel-pattern detection is a canonical Discourse-context-needed case. A Phase-2 discourse-aware validator could lift some EP-5 candidates into mechanical resolution by encoding:

- **Member-uniformity metrics.** Compute member-length variance, lemma-class consistency, and surface-marker repetition rate across the stack. High uniformity + high marker-repetition → high parallel-pattern confidence → mechanical STACK per J1.
- **Register-frame classification.** Tag the surrounding discourse frame as didactic / sermonic / narrative / negative-existential / AICTP-bound. Didactic + uniform stack → STACK; narrative or negative-existential frame → MERGE.
- **Liturgical-formula recognition.** Tag corpus-attested fixed triads (*faith / hope / charity*, *patience / mercy / long-suffering*) for direct match; when the triad appears in didactic register, mechanical STACK; otherwise consult the register-frame classification.

With this infrastructure, the EP-5 candidate population could shift to ~80-90% mechanically resolved, with the residual genuine-ambiguity cases routed to REVIEW. This is future work; for now Category B + REVIEW is the honest output.

### Closed-list indicator refinement

The current `MORAL_QUALITY_LEMMAS_EP5` list is non-exhaustive and based on the pre-2026-04-19 corpus survey. A systematic corpus sweep would refine the list with the full inventory of virtue/vice lemmas, with frequency counts per book and per register. Vice-list lemmas in particular are under-enumerated in the current list (the corpus has dense vice clusters in Mormon 8 / Moroni 9 that warrant systematic enumeration).

The `FORMULAIC_VIRTUE_TRIADS` list currently includes only the *faith / hope / charity* triad as a corpus-attested fixed unit. A future audit could promote additional triads (*patience / mercy / long-suffering*; the divine-attribute triads in Mosiah 4 and Alma 26) to the formulaic-triad list if they demonstrate the same liturgical-formula stability across multiple corpus instances.

### Cross-corpus instantiation

When the apparatus extends to GNT, the same virtue/vice list form will appear with much higher density (the Pauline catalogues are GNT's native habitat). A sister rule (GNT EP-5 or equivalent) would instantiate the same pattern-detection-vs-default-merge logic for Greek coordinate-noun stacks, with closed-list indicators drawn from Pauline-corpus virtue/vice lemmas (*pistis* / *elpis* / *agapē*; *porneia* / *akatharsia* / *aselgeia*; etc.). Similarly Tanakh's virtue-language (*hesed* / *emet* / *tsedeq* / *mishpat*) would warrant its own per-corpus instantiation.

The apparatus's universal pattern — detect formal-parallel markers, route to J1 if present, default to merge if absent — is portable across languages; the closed-list indicators are language-specific.

### N=2 vs. N≥3 boundary cases

The current EP-5 framing routes N=2 cases to §1.9 N=2 Adjudication (M1 synonymy test) and N≥3 cases to J1 (N=3+ cliff). The boundary between N=2 cognate pairs and N=2 distinct-member pairs is sometimes contested — *faith and hope* in some contexts reads as M1 cognate pair (cognate-virtue bonding); in other contexts it reads as distinct-member doublet warranting J1 split. A future audit could refine the N=2 boundary treatment specifically for virtue/vice pairs, possibly adding a sub-clause to §1.9 for moral-quality N=2 cases.

### Punctuation-audit follow-on

The EP-5 corpus's virtue/vice stacks frequently appear with comma-separated members in the source text. Following EP-1's punctuation-audit methodology (2026-03-24), a similar audit could be run on virtue/vice stack environments — strip the commas, re-evaluate the break on grammatical/structural grounds, and surface punctuation-artifact breaks vs. genuine parallel-pattern breaks. This audit has not yet been conducted for EP-5; it is a future work item.
