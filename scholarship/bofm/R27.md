# R27: "Insomuch That" Binding — Scholarship Companion

**Operational entry:** see `readers-bofm/private/01-method/colometry-canon.md §5 R27` (current).

**Status:** This is the scholarship companion documenting WHY R27 exists, HOW we know it's correctly framed, and what intellectual / empirical history grounds its current shape. The operational canon entry says WHAT the rule does; this document says why.

---

## Statement

R27 ("Insomuch That" Binding) governs the consecutive-result subordinator *insomuch that* — the compound mark that ties a result clause to its matrix as a degree-consequence. The default is SPLIT: a consecutive-result clause earns its own line because it occupies a distinct cognitive frame (the consequence) from the matrix predication (the cause). The default is overridden only when the result clause is short enough (≤8 words), shares the matrix subject (continuity), AND fits the same cognitive frame as the matrix (no camera-angle shift). All three conditions must hold for merge; failure of any one returns to the default split.

The rule encodes one of the apparatus's tighter precedence claims: when the subordinator is the compound *insomuch that*, R27 governs over R7 (Purpose Clauses Break) regardless of whether the result clause's verbal nucleus contains a modal auxiliary. The compound subordinator IS the mark of consecutive-result reading.

## Rationale

*Insomuch that* is a consecutive (result) subordinator — etymologically *in + so much + that*, functionally analogous to Latin *adeo...ut* and Greek *ὥστε*. It retroactively assigns a degree reading to the main verb: the main action was done to such an extent that the result followed. The construction's binding is intermediate in the apparatus's typology of subordinators:

- Tighter than pure purpose clauses (R7 — finite *that* + modal): purpose clauses always split because the telos is a separate cognitive frame from the action.
- Looser than correlative *so...that* (where *so* is a co-anchor in the matrix establishing the degree to which the result clause supplies the consequent): correlatives are typically merged because the matrix anchor demands consequent completion.

The intermediate binding is the reason for the three-condition merge test. The construction is not always merge-worthy (it has its own finite verbal nucleus and routinely introduces a new entity or scene); but it is sometimes merge-worthy (the construction is degree-modifying its matrix predication and can fit one cognitive frame when short and subject-continuous). The three conditions discriminate the two cases mechanically.

**Why not always split?** Some *insomuch that* clauses are essentially intensifiers — *"they were exceedingly fearful insomuch that they fell"* — where the result clause names a single co-referential consequence within the same scene. Forcing a split here would over-fragment an atomic thought.

**Why not always merge?** Many *insomuch that* clauses introduce a new entity, a new scene, or a measurable expansion of the matrix — *"he did minister unto them, insomuch that his whole household were converted unto the Lord."* The result clause carries its own predication, a fresh subject, and a camera angle that follows the matrix's consequence forward. Merging here would compress two cognitive frames into one line.

The three conditions are the discriminator. **Word count** (≤8) catches the over-extended cases; **subject continuity** catches the new-entity introductions; **camera-angle absence** catches the scene-progression cases that the first two conditions might miss.

## Grammatical grounding

The grammatical-structural distinction between purpose and consecutive-result is standard in English-grammatical reference works and cross-linguistic typology:

- **CGEL Chapter 11** on adverbial clauses, particularly the section distinguishing purposive *that*-clauses (telic — *"that he might preach"*) from consecutive-result *that*-clauses (degree-consequence — *"so X that Y"*). The two constructions share the simple *that* subordinator in modern English but encode different semantic relations.
- **CGEL Chapter 11** on consecutive-result clauses specifically: *so X that Y* / *such X that Y* constructions are degree-scoping adverbials where the *that*-clause is the consequence of the matrix's modifier degree.
- **Smyth Greek Grammar §2257ff.** on Greek *ὥστε* + infinitive/finite — the Koine consecutive-result subordinator that BoFM's *insomuch that* parallels structurally. *ὥστε* clauses are routinely placed on their own colon in ancient codex layouts (Vaticanus, Sinaiticus) when they carry their own finite predication, but merge with the matrix when they read as a single consequence-of-degree composite.
- **Latin** *adeo...ut* — the consecutive-result construction where *adeo* (matrix degree-adverb) anchors *ut* (consequence-marker). The structural parallel to BoFM's *insomuch that* is etymologically transparent: *insomuch* = *adeo* (matrix degree); *that* = *ut* (consequence-marker). The compound subordinator collapses the two-token correlative into a single mark.

The compound-subordinator nature of *insomuch that* — etymologically *in + so much + that* — is critical for the rule's precedence claim over R7. In UD terms, the mark is the compound, not the simple *that*. R7's UD signature requires `mark.lemma = that` simple, so the compound subordinator naturally falls outside R7's trigger. The interesting case is when the *insomuch that* result clause contains a modal auxiliary (*might*, *should*, *could*) that would otherwise fit R7's signature if the mark were simple *that*. R27 governs: the modal in *insomuch that + MODAL* belongs to the consecutive-result semantics (*"to such an extent that X might happen"*) rather than purposive telic semantics, despite the English reading sometimes permitting a purposive gloss.

## Empirical evidence

### Corpus-wide distribution (2026-04-19 status)

175 *insomuch that* instances total across v2-mine:
- 125 split (71%)
- 50 merged (29%)

The 71/29 distribution at codification time was not yet a clean adoption signal per framework §7.8 (≥80% clean categorization required). The rule was codified as **proposed** pending the first sweep with refined condition 2 (subject-continuity evaluated against the semantic subject in expletive-*there* cases). The validator and applier are mature; a recent merge sweep transaction (`validators/.tx/rule_27_merge_20260510-105021.json`) records the most recent application activity.

### Expletive-*there* sub-clause

The first-pass condition 2 (subject continuity) misfired on expletive-*there* constructions because the surface `nsubj` of *there were many slain* is the expletive *there*, not the semantic subject *many slain*. Without the refinement, condition 2 would mechanically pass on every expletive-*there* result clause (the expletive trivially matches the matrix's expletive or pronominal subject), producing spurious merges.

The refinement evaluates condition 2 against the **semantic subject** — the NP following the BE-verb after expletive *there*. New-entity semantic subjects (the vast majority of corpus cases) fail condition 2 → default SPLIT. Rare continuing-entity semantic subjects (*there was the same man as before*) may pass condition 2; in those cases condition 1 (word count) is typically decisive.

This refinement was the heuristic-ambiguous case the validator flagged as REVIEW-REQUIRED before the 2026-04-19 PM refinement, and the case the canon §6 "validator output is a work queue" guidance cites by name.

### Chained *insomuch that* — Alma 24:2 canonical case

Alma 24:2 — *"And their hatred became exceedingly sore against them, / even insomuch that they began to rebel against their king, / insomuch that they would not that he should be their king"* — is the canonical chained *insomuch that* case. Three lines, each atomic. Each consecutive subordinator introduces a fresh finite predication with its own degree-specification:

- L1: matrix predication (hatred became sore)
- L2: first consecutive-result of L1 (they began to rebel — pairwise against L1)
- L3: second consecutive-result of L2 (they would not have him as king — pairwise against L2, not L1)

The 3-condition merge test applies pairwise (L2 against L1; L3 against L2), but in practice chained instances rarely pass all three conditions pairwise because the camera angle shifts with each degree-intensification. This is a corpus-attested pattern, not a stipulation.

### Result-clause internal structure firing J1/J5

The 3-condition merge test governs the OUTER boundary only. When R27's outer adjudication produces MERGE, the result clause's internal structure can still fire structural justifications to generate breaks INSIDE the merged unit:

- **J5 (substantive adjunct as own focus)** — a fronted substantive temporal PP (*"in the night before he cometh"*), locative PP, or causal PP within the merged result clause earns its own line.
- **J1 (formally-marked parallel series)** — coordinate finite predications within the result clause at N≥3 fall under the J1 cliff.

Evaluating result-clause internals as a separate step after R27's outer adjudication is the discipline. This preserves R27's outer mechanical verdict while letting the framework's generative principle operate on the merged unit's internals.

### Precedence with R7

The precedence claim — *R27 wins over R7 when the subordinator is the compound insomuch that* — was codified during R27's proposal phase (2026-04-19). The decision was non-trivial because *insomuch that + MODAL* superficially fits R7's simple-*that* + modal signature; the codification clarified that the modal in *insomuch that + MODAL* belongs to consecutive-result semantics, not purposive telic semantics. The compound subordinator IS the mark of consecutive-result reading.

The precedence is recorded in BoFM canon §3.5 Tier 5 (R7 yields to R27) and in BoFM canon §3.5.1 *that*-cluster precedence (R27 is checked before R7 in the most-specific-first detector ordering).

## Intellectual lineage

R27 derives from the broader **consecutive-result subordinator family** identified across classical-language scholarship:

- **Greek** *ὥστε* + finite or infinitive — the consecutive-result construction in Koine, routinely set on its own colon in ancient codex layouts when carrying its own finite predication.
- **Latin** *adeo...ut* — the consecutive-result construction with matrix degree-adverb (*adeo*) anchoring the consequence-marker (*ut*). Classical-Latin colometric practice treats the *ut*-clause as a separate sense-line when it carries its own finite verb.
- **Codex Vaticanus (B/03, ~325 CE)** and **Codex Sinaiticus** per-cola layouts of the Greek NT: finite *ὥστε* clauses appear on their own cola when they carry their own predication; they merge with the matrix when they read as a single consequence-of-degree composite. This is the historical precedent for the three-condition merge test's discriminating function.
- **TEI Guidelines** P5 §16 on linguistic-corpus annotation of subordinate clauses, including the consecutive vs. purpose distinction for adverbial subordinators.

The compound subordinator *insomuch that* is a BoFM-archaic-English realization of the consecutive-result family. The KJV uses the same construction; early-modern English preserved the etymological transparency (*in + so much + that*) that modern English largely abandoned in favor of *so X that Y* and *such X that Y* correlatives. The BoFM-archaic register's preference for *insomuch that* over the modern correlatives is consistent with its broader KJV-derived syntactic profile.

The sibling readers-gnt canon's equivalent rule (Greek consecutive *ὥστε*) and readers-tanakh canon's equivalent rules (Hebrew consecutive *'ad ki* / *lema'an*) are sister instantiations of the same universal consecutive-result-subordinator principle, each calibrated to their target language's specific morphology.

R27 sits in the broader **finite-clausal-adverbial split** family with R6 (causal *because*) and R7 (purpose *that* + modal). Each finite adverbial clause earns its own atomic frame by default; the differentiation between the three rules is the mark inventory and the cognitive function (cause / telos / consequence), not a deeper structural difference. R27 differs from R6 and R7 in carrying a three-condition merge override — because the consecutive-result construction's intermediate binding (between purpose-tight and correlative-loose) admits cases where the result clause IS a single cognitive frame with the matrix.

## Adversarial history

R27 was proposed 2026-04-19. The proposal phase has produced material refinements:

### 1. Initial codification (2026-04-19 AM)

Three-condition merge test (word count ≤8, subject continuity, no camera-angle shift), with the default direction SPLIT. The rule was codified as **proposed** pending corpus-wide sweep verification (framework §7.8 — adoption requires ≥80% clean categorization).

Adversarial concerns raised at codification:

- **Risk: condition 3 (camera-angle shift) is not mechanically detectable.** Resolution: condition 3 is flagged for REVIEW when the validator cannot resolve it from surface signals (subject change, scene shift, expletive-*there* introduction). This is acknowledged in the validator implementation (the UD validator's bucket logic includes a REVIEW-REQUIRED bucket for subject-continuity ambiguity).
- **Risk: precedence with R7 not explicit.** Resolution: precedence claim added (R27 wins over R7 when the subordinator is the compound *insomuch that*), recorded in BoFM canon §3.5 Tier 5 and BoFM canon §3.5.1 *that*-cluster precedence.

### 2. Expletive-*there* sub-clause refinement (2026-04-19 PM)

First-pass condition 2 misfired on expletive-*there* constructions. The expletive *there* surface-matches as nsubj continuity with any matrix pronominal/expletive subject. The refinement evaluates condition 2 against the **semantic subject** — the NP following the BE-verb. New-entity semantic subjects fail condition 2 → default SPLIT.

This refinement was the heuristic-ambiguous case the validator originally flagged as REVIEW-REQUIRED. After refinement, the expletive-*there* sub-clause is mechanically resolvable for the vast majority of corpus cases.

### 3. Chained *insomuch that* sub-clause codification

Alma 24:2 — *"hatred became sore... insomuch that they began to rebel... insomuch that they would not have him as king"* — surfaced the chained-asyndetic pattern. The codification clarified that the 3-condition merge test applies pairwise (each *insomuch that* against its immediate antecedent, not against the top-level matrix), and that chained instances rarely pass all three conditions pairwise because camera angle typically shifts with each degree-intensification.

### 4. Result-clause internal-structure carve-out

The 3-condition test governs the OUTER boundary only. Result-clause internals fire J1/J5 separately after R27's outer adjudication. This was codified to prevent R27's outer MERGE verdict from suppressing legitimate internal breaks (e.g., a fronted substantive temporal PP within a merged *insomuch that* result clause earning its own line per J5).

### 5. UD validator maturation

The UD validator (`validate_rule_27_ud.py`) replaced the regex precursor (`validate_rule_27_insomuch_that.py`). The UD version handles two tokenizations of the compound subordinator:

- **Sig-A:** insomuch=ADV/advmod + that=SCONJ/mark, both children of advcl head.
- **Sig-B:** insomuch=ADV/mark + that=SCONJ/fixed(head=insomuch), insomuch child of advcl head.

Plus rare single-MWE token form. Both are handled by the validator's `_is_insomuch_that()` discriminator. The word-count fix (condition 1) counts only non-PUNCT tokens in the advcl subtree that sit on the same v2-mine line as the *insomuch* mark, mirroring the regex validator's same-line measurement approach.

A recent merge sweep transaction (`validators/.tx/rule_27_merge_20260510-105021.json`) records corpus-wide application activity, but the formal status-promotion (proposed → active) per §7.8 awaits documented ≥80% clean-categorization evidence in the commit record.

## Future work

### Adoption per framework §7.8

The rule remains **proposed**. Framework §7.8 requires the first corpus sweep to produce ≥80% clean categorization (unambiguous SPLIT or MERGE without REVIEW residue) before promotion to active. The 2026-04-19 status (71% split, 29% merged) predates the expletive-*there* refinement and the UD validator's mature condition-2 evaluation. A status promotion is contingent on a documented post-refinement sweep meeting the threshold.

When the sweep clears, the operational entry's Status field updates Proposed → Active, audit-evidence is captured in the commit, and the canon §3 quick-reference table drops the *(proposed)* annotation.

### Condition 3 (camera-angle shift) — Phase 2 mechanization

Condition 3 is currently REVIEW-tier when the validator cannot resolve it from surface signals. Phase 2 of the apparatus, if extended with discourse-entity tracking and scene-progression detection, would let camera-angle shift be mechanically resolved by attending to subject-entity continuity, locative/temporal scene-marker continuity, and predicate-class continuity across the boundary. This is future infrastructure work shared with the petition-frame ambiguity filter (R17) and the cataphoric/anaphoric *that*-discrimination (R19).

### Cross-corpus consistency

The readers-gnt canon's Greek-consecutive-*ὥστε* rule and readers-tanakh canon's Hebrew-consecutive (*'ad ki* / *lema'an*) rules are sister instantiations of the same universal consecutive-result-subordinator principle. As the apparatus extends to additional corpora (e.g., Vulgate finite *ita...ut*, Quran consecutive *fa-*), the consecutive-result-subordinator rule's instantiation pattern stabilizes:

- Identify the target language's compound or correlative consecutive-result subordinator(s).
- Identify the target language's purpose-vs-consecutive discriminator (modal marking, infinitival marking, dedicated subordinator alternation).
- Apply the same default-split-with-three-condition-merge logic with the language's syntactic inventory.

The three conditions themselves (word count, subject continuity, camera-angle absence) are universal — they encode atomic-thought test discriminators rather than language-specific morphology — so cross-corpus portability is expected.

### Word-count threshold tuning

The current condition 1 threshold (≤8 non-PUNCT word tokens) is grammatical-completeness-driven. A future audit could verify per-corpus-attested clean-merge cases against the threshold and tune it (raise it slightly to capture additional short-result merges; lower it slightly to tighten the merge filter against camera-angle-shifting longer results). The 2026-04-19 status (71/29 distribution) is consistent with the current threshold; a future post-refinement audit could revisit.

### Petition-frame ambiguity boundary (shared with R17)

A small subset of *insomuch that* clauses under speech- and volition-class matrix verbs may interact with R17's PETITION_FRAME_VERBS routing. The boundary is not currently a known REVIEW-tier hot-spot for R27 (the compound subordinator is its own mark and routes cleanly to R27 before R17 considers complement-integrity), but a future audit could verify the routing handles the edge cases (e.g., *"he prayed insomuch that..."* — does R17's petition-frame ambiguity filter route this, or does R27's compound-mark routing pre-empt? Current detector ordering — BoFM canon §3.5.1 has R27 at position 4, before R7 at position 5 but the petition-frame filter is a sub-clause within R17 at position 3 — should pre-empt R17, but the interaction warrants confirmation.).
