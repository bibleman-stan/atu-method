# R18a: Patriarch-Deity-Triad Fixed Formula — Scholarship Companion

**Operational entry:** see `readers-bofm/private/01-method/colometry-canon.md §5 R18a`.

**Status:** This is the scholarship companion documenting WHY R18a exists, HOW we know it is correctly framed, and what intellectual and empirical history grounds its current shape. The operational canon entry says WHAT the rule does; this document says why.

---

## Statement

R18a (Patriarch-Deity-Triad Fixed Formula) requires that the deity-formula token sequence `God of Abraham` ... `Isaac` ... `Jacob` be kept whole on a single line whenever the spanning sequence appears within a single verse-block, regardless of which of its six attested distribution variants the surface form takes. The rule is a sister to R18 (fixed lexicalized idioms), R1 (AICTP formula), and R23 (date-colophon formulas) — each governs a closed-list KEEP_WHOLE span in canon §3.5 Tier 2.

## Rationale

The triad *God of Abraham, God of Isaac, God of Jacob* — and its compressed variants — functions in the BoFM register (as in the KJV register and the underlying Septuagintal Greek register before it) as a **single fixed referring expression to YHWH**, not as an enumeration of three deities. Severing the formula across line boundaries fractures a unitary deity-reference into the apparent compositional enumeration of three patriarch-anchored deities — a parsing that the formula's lexicalized status forbids.

The principle is identical to R18's: a multi-word frozen expression that functions as a single retrieval unit in the reader's mental lexicon should be presented as one visual unit. What distinguishes R18a from R18 is the **variant-matching mechanism**: where R18's closed-list members are exact token sequences (any line containing *put to death* matches), R18a's surface forms permit free distribution of the `the God of` anchor across the three patriarchs:

- *the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob* (fully-distributed)
- *the God of Abraham, and the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob* (fully-distributed with leading *and*)
- *the God of Abraham, and of Isaac, and the God of Jacob* (partial *of*-compression)
- *the God of Abraham and Isaac and of Jacob* (asyndetic *of*-mixed)
- *the God of Abraham, and Isaac, and Jacob* (anchor-shared)
- *even the God of Abraham, and the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob* (with leading intensifier)

The operational matcher therefore uses an in-order spanning-sequence rule rather than exact-string lookup: any line containing `God of Abraham` followed by `Isaac` followed by `Jacob` is treated as the formula.

## Grammatical grounding

Syntactically, the patriarch-deity-triad is a **fixed appositive-coordinate referring expression** functioning as a complex proper noun. Each member surface-parses as a head NP (*the God*) with a PP modifier (*of Abraham*, etc.), and the three NPs surface as a coordinate compound:

- UD parse: typically three `nmod` / `conj` chains with `of` as the `case`-marker preposition; the three NPs joined by `cc` / `conj`.
- Surface morphology: the `the God of` anchor is freely elidable from coordinate members (English allows ellipsis of repeated head + modifier-stem in coordinate noun phrases — *the King of England, France, and Spain* is a stylistic alternative to *the King of England, the King of France, and the King of Spain*).

But the semantic-pragmatic function of the unit is unitary: it is a **fixed appellation for YHWH**, parallel to *Lord of Hosts* (one-deity, multi-word title) or *King of kings* (one-referent compound). The KJV English register inherits this from the Hebrew Bible's covenantal formula אֱלֹהֵי אַבְרָהָם אֱלֹהֵי יִצְחָק וֵאלֹהֵי יַעֲקֹב (Exodus 3:6, 3:15, 3:16, 4:5; Matthew 22:32 in the NT). The compositional surface parse — "three different deities, one per patriarch" — is grammatically licensed but semantically wrong; the formulaic-retrieval reading — "the LORD, identified by the covenantal-patriarch genealogy" — is the canonical interpretation.

The Layer 3 placement of R18a (rather than Layer 1) reflects the fact that the indivisibility is an editorial recognition of the formula's lexical-bundle status in the BoFM-KJV-LXX register, not a generic English-grammatical fact. Modern English-language Bible translations vary in their typographic treatment of the formula (some preserve full distribution as a stylistic choice; others compress); the apparatus's KEEP_WHOLE mandate is principled with respect to the formula's status as a unitary referring expression in the source register.

## Empirical evidence

### Corpus distribution

The deity-triad formula appears at eight loci in the BoFM corpus:

1. **1 Nephi 6:4** — fully-distributed; post-codification: merged
2. **1 Nephi 19:10** — partially-distributed (*of*-compressed middle member); already compliant
3. **Mosiah 7:19** — anchor-shared (no repetition of *God of*); already compliant
4. **Mosiah 23:24** — asyndetic-*of*-mixed; already compliant
5. **Alma 29:11** — fully-distributed with leading intensifier (*the Lord God,*); post-codification: merged
6. **Alma 36:2** — fully-distributed; already compliant
7. **3 Nephi 4:30** — fully-distributed; already compliant
8. **Mormon 9:11** — fully-distributed; post-codification: merged

Post-codification (commit `2e4767e`, 2026-05-11), the corpus is uniformly compliant: three previously-split instances were merged to single lines per R18a; five instances were already on single lines (some as single-line fully-distributed shapes, others as compressed shapes).

The patriarch personal-name list (lacking the `God of` anchor) appears at two loci (1 Nephi 17:40, Alma 7:25) and is governed by SCOPE-exclusion #1 of R18a — these are coordinate-NP-object lists referring to the three persons, not the deity, and default-merge per canon §1.9 (coordinate predications only earn N≥3 stacking).

### Compliance verification

The validator `validators/colometry/validate_rule_18a_patriarch_triad.py` (surface-pattern, verse-block-scoped) reports zero violations against the post-codification corpus.

## Intellectual lineage

R18a derives from the same **lexical-bundle integrity principle** that grounds R18 (see [`R18.md`](R18.md)), instantiated for the specific case of fixed appellation formulas referring to deity. The principle has cross-tradition support:

- **Septuagintal-LXX manuscript layout traditions:** the triadic deity-formula in Exodus and the NT quotations is sometimes presented as a single column-line in early codices (e.g., Codex Vaticanus's per-cola arrangement of Matthew 22:32 keeps the formula whole).
- **TEI Guidelines** P5 on multi-word expressions (`<mwe>`, `<choice>`): scholarly-editing standard for tagging frozen multi-word formulas as units.
- **Targum-tradition translation discipline:** the rabbinic Aramaic targumim consistently render the Hebrew triadic-deity formula as a single appellation, never as three separate deity-references — corroborating the unitary-referring-expression reading from the source-language tradition.
- **CGEL** Chapter 4 on complex transitive constructions with idiomatic complement and Chapter 12 on coordination-with-ellipsis: the grammatical mechanics of *the God of X, of Y, of Z* coordination admit free distribution of the anchor without affecting the unitary-reference reading.

R18a's codification (2026-05-11) was prompted by Stan's observation that the corpus exhibited inconsistent treatment of the formula across its eight loci — some instances were split into three lines (one *God of X* per line), others merged into a single line. The split treatments produced what the canon §1 generative principle would otherwise produce — each *God of X* as its own apparent proposition — but the principle's output here was overridden by the formula's lexical-bundle status. Codifying R18a as a Tier 2 KEEP_WHOLE rule resolves the inconsistency and aligns BoFM treatment with the source-tradition discipline.

## Adversarial history

The codification of R18a was bundled with a parallel hostile audit on a separate but conversationally-adjacent question: *does the corpus admit N≥3 coordinate-object ellipsis stacking under a shared verb*, as in Alma 29:5 (*whether he desireth good or evil, life or death, joy or remorse of conscience*)?

The audit's Verdict H5: **no clean mechanical discriminator exists.** Falsifying corpus evidence:

- **Helaman 4:11** — *murdering, plundering, lying, stealing, committing adultery* — N=5 ellipsis-recoverable predication stack, canonically MERGED on a single line. Defeats any candidate discriminator based on image-completeness, semantic distinctness, or polysyndeton-vs-asyndeton.
- **Alma 40:5** — *one time, or a second time, or a third time* (under shared `whether there shall be ___`) — N=3 *or*-coordinated ellipsis, canonically MERGED. Defeats the candidate discriminator based on internal-coordination of members.
- **Alma 29:5** itself — the verse whose stacking the audit was testing — is a corpus singleton for the "internal bipartite-antithetic *X or Y* coordinated members × N≥3" pattern. Building a rule on a singleton fails canon §7.3's biased-spot-check guard.

The verdict's bearing on R18a: the patriarch-deity-triad's unitary-reference status is **not** an ellipsis-stacking-eligibility argument. The three *God of X* members do ellipsis-recover (*the God of Abraham, [the God] of Isaac, [the God] of Jacob*), but the rule that protects them is KEEP_WHOLE (Tier 2 indivisibility), not a J1 structural-justification stacking license. The audit confirmed that no coordinate-object ellipsis-stacking license exists in the canon at any N≥3, full stop — and R18a correctly takes the indivisibility path rather than attempting a stacking-eligibility framing that would have over-extended the canon.

This is the second adversarial-audit cycle the formula has been through. The first (pre-codification) considered whether to handle the triad as an M4 protected-reference under canon §1's compressed-triad framing; that path was rejected in favor of the cleaner R18-sibling fixed-formula framing — easier to validate mechanically, easier to extend to sister formulas (the GNT's *Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob* triads at Matthew 22:32, Acts 3:13, etc.; the Tanakh's source-language formulas in Exodus).

## Future work

### Closed-list completeness audit (BoFM)

R18a's scope is the patriarch-deity-triad specifically. Other fixed deity-formulas in the BoFM register may warrant analogous sister rules. Candidates for future review include:

- *the Lord of Hosts* — KJV-derived; functions as a fixed appellation. May fall under R18's existing closed list rather than warranting a separate sister rule.
- *the Lamb of God* / *the Lamb that was slain* — Christological titles with formulaic status.
- *the great and last sacrifice* / *the great and last day* — BoFM-distinctive eschatological formulas.

Each candidate would require corpus-distribution survey + lexical-bundle status verification before codification. The R18a precedent establishes the codification pattern.

### Cross-corpus instantiation (GNT, Tanakh)

The sister readers-gnt and readers-tanakh canons will require analogous patriarch-triad rules with corpus-specific scope:

- **GNT:** the triad appears at Matthew 22:32 (= Mark 12:26, Luke 20:37), Acts 3:13, Acts 7:32, and the underlying-quotation contexts of Hebrews 11:8-22. The Greek surface form is τὸν θεὸν Ἀβραὰμ καὶ τὸν θεὸν Ἰσαὰκ καὶ τὸν θεὸν Ἰακώβ (Matt 22:32) with attested distribution variants paralleling the BoFM's English variants.
- **Tanakh:** the source formula אֱלֹהֵי אַבְרָהָם אֱלֹהֵי יִצְחָק וֵאלֹהֵי יַעֲקֹב appears in Exodus 3:6, 3:15, 3:16, 4:5, with additional patriarch-deity formula loci across Genesis, 1 Chronicles, and the prophetic books.

Each sister canon will codify the formula via its own R18a-analog with corpus-appropriate surface-pattern matchers. The framework (Tier 2 KEEP_WHOLE indivisibility for unitary deity-formulas) is universal; the per-corpus surface inventories are not.

### Validator UD-query implementation

R18a's current validator is surface-pattern only (`validate_rule_18a_patriarch_triad.py`). A UD-query implementation would integrate with the broader UD-pattern dashboard and afford more rigorous tokenization at edge cases. Defer until UD-parse coverage of the triad's coordinate-NP structure is empirically stable across the corpus.
