# R1: AICTP Formula Integrity — Scholarship Companion

**Operational entry:** see `readers-bofm/private/01-method/colometry-canon.md §5 Rule 1` (current) or the proposed restructured draft at [`_drafts/R1-operational.md`](_drafts/R1-operational.md).

**Status:** This is the scholarship companion documenting WHY R1 exists, HOW we know it is correctly framed, and what intellectual and empirical history grounds its current shape. The operational canon entry says WHAT the rule does; this document says why.

---

## Statement

R1 (AICTP Formula Integrity) requires that the fixed extraposition formula *And it came to pass* — and its closed-list variants (*And now it came to pass*, *And it shall come to pass*) — be kept whole on a single line. The formula is treated as a single lexicalized unit; no line break is permitted internal to its token sequence. The trailing *that* (when present) is governed by sister rule R16, which mandates a break before *that* so that *that* leads its content clause.

## Rationale

*And it came to pass* is the BoFM's most pervasive narrative-frame formula — a fixed multi-word lexical unit that signals scene transition. Splitting the formula internally produces no valid atomic-thought reading: *"And it came to"* dangles as a bare prepositional fragment; *"pass that..."* begins with a participle stranded from its formulaic context. Neither half is a proposition, and neither half functions as a formally-marked atomic unit under any of the five structural justifications.

The formula's indivisibility is therefore a syntax-veto case under `framework.md §1.2.3` (formula integrity): a lexicalized multi-word frame that functions as a single unit. Any split the generative principle would otherwise produce internal to the formula is blocked by R1's KEEP_WHOLE mandate.

The formula's right-edge boundary handling (where *that* falls) is a separate matter and is handled by R16. R1 and R16 are coupled — R1 protects the formula's internal span; R16 governs the break placement immediately after — and the two rules together produce the apparatus's canonical AICTP line shape: *"And it came to pass that [content]"* on one line, or, when the content is long, *"And it came to pass / that [content]"* with the break leading *that* into its clause.

## Grammatical grounding

Syntactically, *And it came to pass* is an extraposition construction with:

- **Dummy subject** *it* (UD: `expl(came, it)`) — a non-referential placeholder licensed by the extraposed content clause.
- **Matrix verb** *came* (an aspectual / inchoative use of *come* in the early-modern English of the KJV register).
- **Prepositional phrase** *to pass* — an idiomatic intransitive complement meaning approximately "to happen / to occur."
- **Subordinator** *that* — when present, introduces the extraposed content clause that is the logical subject of the matrix.

This extraposition pattern is treated in CGEL Chapter 16 (extraposition with dummy *it*); the construction's archaic register and lexical fossilization in KJV-derivative English is documented across early-modern-English grammars. The formula is one of the most-recognized lexical bundles in the BoFM and is by tradition treated as a unit in both reading and citation.

The Layer 3 status of R1 (rather than Layer 1) reflects the fact that the formula's indivisibility is an editorial recognition of the lexical-bundle status, not a generic English mid-phrase prohibition. A naïve syntactic parser could in principle accept a break inside *came + to + pass*; the editorial discipline rejects it because the unit is lexicalized as a single discourse-frame marker.

## Empirical evidence

### Corpus prevalence

AICTP and its variants are the BoFM's single most frequent multi-word formula. The combined family appears across all 15 books, with concentrations in 1 Nephi, Mosiah, Alma, Helaman, 3 Nephi, Mormon, and Ether (narrative books). 1 Nephi 1 alone contains 8 AICTP instances (cf. `colometry-canon.md` §6 fixture-coverage table).

### Compliance

The v2-mine corpus is uniformly compliant with R1 — every AICTP token-sequence occurrence is whole on its line. No corpus violations have ever been reported under R1's KEEP_WHOLE mandate. The validator at `validators/colometry/validate_rule_01_ud.py` reports zero violations across the corpus.

The compliance rate is unsurprising: AICTP indivisibility was one of the earliest editorial settlements in the project's history and has been enforced from the first hand-edited v2 source files. The rule codifies a discipline that was already universal in practice.

### Coupling with R16

The coupling between R1 (formula integrity) and R16 (dangling-*that*) was settled early. R16 mandates that *that* lead its clause rather than trail the formula, so the canonical AICTP shape is either:

- *"And it came to pass that [short content]"* — all on one line when content is short enough to keep atomic; or
- *"And it came to pass / that [long content]"* — R16 break placement when content is too long to keep one-line.

In either case, R1's formula-internal span (*And it came to pass*) is whole; R16 handles the boundary on the right.

### Slot-filler interaction (J5)

When AICTP is followed by a substantive temporal, locative, or causal PP that fills one of the formula's projected slots (when, where, why), J5 (substantive adjunct as own focus) earns the PP its own line without violating R1. The canonical example is Alma 52:18: *"And it came to pass that Moroni did arrive with his army at the land of Bountiful, / in the latter end of the twenty and seventh year of the reign of the judges over the people of Nephi."* The temporal PP is 15 words — substantively long enough to be its own focus, and grammatically peripheral to the matrix predication (*Moroni did arrive*). R1's formula-internal span remains intact; J5 governs the post-content break.

This interaction is documented at `colometry-canon.md` §1 J5 (the five-structural-justifications closed list) and is referenced in R1's exclusions.

## Intellectual lineage

R1 derives from the broader **formula-integrity principle** in scholarly editing: a lexicalized multi-word frame that functions as a single discourse marker should be presented as one visual unit. The principle predates the apparatus and appears in different forms in:

- **Codex Vaticanus (B/03) and Codex Sinaiticus** per-cola layouts of the Greek NT: lexicalized formulas (e.g., *καὶ ἐγένετο* — the GNT cognate of AICTP) are treated as single-cola units in the ancient codex tradition.
- **TEI Guidelines** P5 on lexical-bundle annotation: multi-word expressions with frozen semantic content are tagged as units.
- **KJV-derivative typesetting traditions** that paragraph at AICTP boundaries: the formula's discourse-frame status was recognized in 19th-century stereotype editions of the BoFM.

The sibling GNT canon's rule on *καὶ ἐγένετο* formula integrity, and Tanakh's rule on wayyiqtol narrative formulas, are sister instantiations of the same universal formula-integrity principle (see `framework.md §1.2.3`). Each per-corpus canon enumerates its own formula inventory because formulas are language-specific lexical bundles; the universal principle is consistent across canons.

The Layer 3 placement of R1 (rather than absorbing it into Layer 1) is principled: Layer 1 codifies generic English syntactic prohibitions that apply to all texts; R1 codifies the editorial recognition that *And it came to pass* is a lexicalized formula in the BoFM-specific register. A different English text could legitimately split *came / to pass* if the words were not lexically bundled; in the BoFM, they are.

## Adversarial history

R1 has not been subjected to substantive adversarial audit cycles because the rule's content is uncontroversial: the formula is universally recognized as a lexical bundle in the BoFM editorial tradition, and corpus compliance has been complete from the first v2-mine edition. The rule was codified in the earliest tranche of canon rules and has not required refinement.

The one substantive adjacent dispute was the placement of trailing *that*: should *that* line-trail the formula (*"And it came to pass that"* on one line, with content beginning on the next) or line-lead its content (*"And it came to pass"* on one line, *"that [content]"* on the next)? This was settled in favor of R16's line-lead discipline — *that* leads its clause — and the coupling between R1 and R16 was formalized as part of §3.5 Tier 1.

No retirement, narrowing, or expansion of R1 has been seriously proposed. The rule's exceptions list is empty (the operational entry's Exclusions are pointer-references to R16 and J5, not exceptions to R1's internal-indivisibility claim).

## Future work

### Variant inventory completeness

The AICTP_VARIANTS closed list currently enumerates three forms: *And it came to pass*, *And now it came to pass*, *And it shall come to pass*. A future audit could verify the inventory is exhaustive — that no fourth variant (e.g., *Now it came to pass* without leading *And*, or *And it had come to pass* perfective form) appears in the corpus with the same lexicalized status. The validator could be extended to flag any matching `expl(came, it)` UD pattern outside the closed list for editorial review, ensuring no novel variant slips by without explicit registration.

### Cross-corpus formula-integrity consistency

As the apparatus extends to additional corpora (Vulgate, Quran, Septuagint, etc.), R1's instantiation pattern stabilizes:

- Identify the target corpus's narrative-frame formulas (closed-list inventory).
- Codify each formula's keep-whole mandate as a per-corpus formula-integrity rule.
- Couple with any boundary-handling sister rules (analogous to R16 for AICTP).

The GNT *καὶ ἐγένετο* rule and Tanakh's wayyiqtol-formula rule are existing sister instantiations; future corpora should follow the same pattern.

### Lexical-bundle generalization

A speculative future direction: codifying a generic Layer-3 lexical-bundle rule that catches any high-frequency multi-word formula in any corpus, parameterized by the per-corpus inventory. This would generalize R1 and its siblings into a single framework principle (`framework.md §1.2.3` already specifies the universal principle) with per-corpus closed-list overrides. The benefit would be uniformity of validator design across canons; the cost would be reduced per-corpus specificity in the canon's surface presentation. Defer until a third or fourth corpus is in scope.
