# R28: Speech-Act Announcement After Frame — Scholarship Companion

**Operational entry:** see `readers-bofm/private/01-method/colometry-canon.md §5 Rule 28` (current) or this migration's proposed restructured entry at [`_drafts/R28-operational.md`](_drafts/R28-operational.md).

**Status:** This is the scholarship companion documenting WHY R28 exists, HOW we know it's correctly framed, and what intellectual / empirical history grounds its current shape. The operational canon entry says WHAT the rule does; this document says why.

---

## Statement

R28 (Speech-Act Announcement After Frame) requires that a matrix speech predication — subject + finite speech verb + colon-introduced direct discourse — be split from a preceding scene-setting adverbial frame when the speech verb's lemma is in the closed list (*say*, *speak*, *declare*, *cry*, *answer*) and the frame is a genuine temporal/causal/locative `advcl` introduced by an unambiguous subordinator (*after*, *when*, *while*, *before*, *since*, *until*, *because*, *though*, *although*, *lest*, *except*). The break is inserted before the leftmost non-PUNCT token of the speech-clause subtree that follows the frame; the frame-closing comma stays on the frame line.

R28 is the BoFM-corpus operational instantiation of J3 (speech-act announcement) at the matrix-predication level — specifically, the sub-case in which the announcement is preceded by a scene-setting adverbial frame on the same v2-mine line.

## Rationale

The frame and the speech-act are two distinct cognitive frames. The adverbial frame sets the scene (when / where / under what conditions the speech occurred); the matrix speech-predication is the discrete communicative event ("the king said"). Merging them onto a single v2-mine line compresses two atomic frames into one — failing the atomic-thought test at the announcement level, where the reader must process both the scene-setup and the announcement before reaching the quoted content.

The break is grammatical-structural rather than rhetorical. The adverbial frame is a syntactically peripheral constituent — an `advcl` attaching outside the matrix's argument structure — and its detachability is cognitively active when the matrix predication is itself a complete communicative act. Speech-act announcements are paradigmatic complete predications (subject + finite verb + colon-introducing direct discourse); they earn their own atomic frame whenever a peripheral constituent precedes them on the same line.

This rule operationalizes the framework's §1.4 J3 structural justification at the matrix-predication granularity. J3 establishes that speech-act announcement and quoted content are separate cognitive frames; R28 names the specific intra-matrix pattern in which a same-line frame-plus-announcement collapses what should be two frames into one.

## Grammatical grounding

The grammatical-structural detachability of adverbial frames from speech-act announcements is standard in English-grammatical and Greek-grammatical reference works:

- **CGEL Chapter 11** on adverbial subordinate clauses: temporal, causal, conditional, and concessive *advcl* clauses are peripheral constituents, syntactically detachable from the matrix without affecting matrix valence. When the matrix is itself a complete communicative act (subject + finite speech-verb + introduced direct discourse), the peripheral frame is cognitively separable.
- **CGEL Chapter 15** on direct vs. indirect discourse: direct-discourse introduction — speech-tag + colon (or its equivalent punctuation) — is a complete communicative predication. The speech-tag is not grammatically dependent on what precedes it; preceding adverbial frames are scene-setters, not arguments of the speech act.
- **Smyth Greek Grammar §2168ff.** on subordinate temporal/causal clauses in narrative: classical Greek prose routinely fronts temporal/causal clauses before speech-tag matrix predications, and per-cola layouts of ancient Greek manuscripts (Codex Vaticanus, Codex Sinaiticus) typically place the speech-tag on its own cola, distinct from the preceding frame.

The result-direction filter (Exclusion 3 in the operational entry) is grounded in the standard grammatical distinction between *frame* (preceding) and *consequence* (following) advcl attachments. When a matrix speech verb precedes an advcl introduced by a result-class subordinator (*that*, *insomuch*, *until*), the advcl is a consequence of the speech act, not a frame for it; conflating these would mis-split a consequence-clause off the matrix it depends on.

The participial-continuation filter (Exclusion 1) reflects the BoFM-archaic-register convention of using *, saying:* as a participial speech-continuation marker — semantically equivalent to a colon, not a scene-setting frame. CGEL Chapter 14's treatment of supplementary participles (here, a present participial *saying* attaching to the matrix speech-VERB) covers the same grammatical territory.

## Empirical evidence

### Initial corpus sweep (2026-05-10)

The UD-query detector was implemented per canon §5 R28 with the closed lists `SPEECH_LEMMAS = {say, speak, declare, cry, answer}` and `FRAME_MARK_LEMMAS = {after, when, while, before, since, until, because, though, although, lest, except}` plus the participial-continuation filter (`ADVCL_EXCLUDED_LEMMAS = {say}` — the *saying* lemma). The first corpus-wide sweep produced 27 STRONG-SPLIT-CANDIDATE instances and 1 REVIEW-REQUIRED case (Alma 34:34 — speech-tag structurally inside the advcl).

Per-book distribution of the 27 splits:

- 1 Nephi: 6
- Enos: 1
- Mosiah: 2
- Alma: 6 (including the *for said he* Ammon-flock passage at Alma 17:29)
- Helaman: 3 (including Hel 13:25 *saith-the-Lord* parenthetical)
- 3 Nephi: 8 (Jesus-discourse frames + *saith-the-Father* / *saith-thy-God* parentheticals)
- Ether: 1

Five of the 27 splits land on *saith-the-Lord* / *saith-the-prophet* / *saith-the-Father* parenthetical patterns. These are the canonical "authentication-stamp" parenthetical — a self-attributive speech-act announcement intruding on a divine-first-person discourse, which is a recognized own-line case per the canon's parenthetical-authentication-stamp principle. The Rule 28 trigger correctly identified these as same-line frame+announcement violations and correctly applied the split.

Adoption per §7.8: 27/28 clean (96.4%) — well above the 80% adoption threshold. The single REVIEW-REQUIRED case (Alma 34:34) was structurally distinct (speech-tag *inside* the advcl, not after it), and the detector correctly demoted it to REVIEW rather than mechanically splitting. The rule moves from Proposed to Active.

### Wave 6 detector refinements (2026-05-10)

Two filter refinements were added during Wave 6 polish to bring the detector's structural precision in line with the rule's grammatical intent:

**Result-direction filter.** Early-implementation tests surfaced cases where a `that`/`insomuch`/`until`-headed advcl following a speech verb was being flagged as a "frame" — when in fact the advcl was a consequence-clause depending on the speech act, not a scene-setter preceding it. Example: *"I did speak many words unto them, that they were pacified"* — the *that*-clause is the result of speaking, not the frame for it. The filter compares token-id order: when `speech_verb.id < advcl.id` AND the advcl mark lemma is in `RESULT_MARK_LEMMAS = {that, insomuch, until}`, the advcl is skipped as out-of-scope. Result-direction inversion was a recurring false-positive shape; the filter eliminated it cleanly.

**Tightened `aux:pass` branch.** An earlier branch admitted any advcl carrying an `aux:pass` dependent as a "frame" (passive participial absolute reading). Audit found this over-permissive: passive participial *advcl* without a temporal/locative mark and without a distinct own-subject is manner-circumstantial (*"being filled with the Spirit, he spake"*) — not a scene-setting frame. The tightened branch now requires the `aux:pass` advcl to ALSO have either a recognized frame mark (handled by the main `FRAME_MARK_LEMMAS` branch) or a true-absolute construction (own `nsubj` differing from the matrix `nsubj`). Manner-circumstantial passive advcl no longer flags.

Both refinements were post-codification audit findings (§7.3 trigger #6 — mechanical-signature changes under a settled rule) and were applied before the 27-instance corpus sweep.

### REVIEW-REQUIRED case (Alma 34:34)

*"Ye cannot say, when ye are brought to that awful crisis,"* — the speech-tag *Ye cannot say* is structurally inside the *when*-clause's surface arrangement; there is no non-PUNCT speech-only token following the rightmost advcl-subtree token on the line. The applier's char-offset computation returned no clean split column. The detector demoted this case from STRONG-SPLIT-CANDIDATE to REVIEW-REQUIRED — correctly, because mechanically inserting a break would either bifurcate the *when*-clause or strand orphan tokens. This case is a genuine canon-gap residual; whether *Ye cannot say* should be split off (and if so, where) is editorial-judgment territory rather than mechanical.

### Saith-the-Lord parenthetical convergence

Five of the 27 corpus applications fell on the *saith-the-Lord* / *saith-the-prophet* / *saith-the-Father* parenthetical pattern (e.g., Helaman 13:25 *"for I have a great work to do, / saith the Lord"*). These are independent of the temporal-frame trigger — they are intra-discourse self-attributive parentheticals — but the detector's structural condition (speech-VERB + nsubj + advcl-sibling on the same line) catches them when a preceding clause is parsed as an advcl. The convergence is mechanically clean: the parenthetical authentication-stamp principle and R28's frame-then-announcement structural condition both prescribe SPLIT on these instances, and the detector mechanically agrees.

## Intellectual lineage

R28 derives from the broader **speech-act-announcement separation principle** — the recognition that direct-discourse introduction is a self-contained communicative predication that earns its own atomic frame regardless of what surrounds it. This principle is older than the apparatus; it appears in:

- **Codex Vaticanus (B/03, ~325 CE) and Codex Sinaiticus per-cola layouts** of the Greek NT: speech-tag matrix predications (*εἶπεν αὐτοῖς*, *ἀπεκρίθη*) routinely receive their own cola, distinct from preceding temporal/causal frame clauses. This is the historical precedent for the speech-act-after-frame break.
- **Modern scholarly editions** of biblical and classical texts that use sense-line presentation typically place speech-tag matrix predications on their own line. The NA28 prose layout and the Skousen sense-line edition of the Book of Mormon both reflect this convention with varying levels of mechanical consistency.
- **CGEL Chapter 11 / Chapter 15** on adverbial detachability and direct-discourse introduction: peripheral *advcl* + speech-act matrix is a standard textbook bifurcation point for clause-level analysis.

R28 sits in the broader **J3 (speech-act announcement)** family with J3's other operational sub-clauses (per-corpus recurring speech-tag formulae, per the framework's §1.4 J3 note about per-corpus-instantiated patterns). The sibling rule in readers-gnt (Greek speech-tag matrix-predication separation from preceding temporal/causal frame, *εἶπεν αὐτοῖς* + frame patterns) and the sibling rule in readers-tanakh (Hebrew speech-tag matrix-predication separation, *wayyomer* + frame patterns) are sister instantiations of the same universal J3 sub-principle, each calibrated to the target language's speech-verb closed list and temporal-subordinator inventory.

The specific BoFM-archaic-register pattern R28 targets — frame-clause + speech-tag with colon-introduction — is inherited from the KJV-derived narrative register the Book of Mormon's English text reproduces. The KJV's frequent use of *and it came to pass that after X had Y, Z said:* constructions is preserved in the BoFM text and produces the corpus's dense same-line frame-plus-announcement collisions that R28 resolves.

## Adversarial history

R28 was proposed on 2026-04-19 and adopted to Active status on 2026-05-10 via a 27-instance corpus sweep (§7.8). The audit cycles producing material refinements:

### 1. Initial codification (2026-04-19)

Proposed under the canon-§5 template (Grammatical basis / UD signature / Diagnostic / Example) with speech-verb closed list `{say, speak, declare, cry, answer}` and frame-mark intuition. The rule was initially under-specified in several ways:

- Frame-mark inventory was prose-described rather than enumerated.
- *as* was ambiguous between temporal (*as he walked*) and comparative (*as a man speaketh*) but no discriminator was specified.
- Participial *saying* was not flagged as an exclusion despite being the dominant BoFM same-line speech-continuation pattern.

### 2. R28 Authorial Asymmetry import (parallel, distinct rule)

The canon also references a *different* "R28" entry — the R28 Authorial Asymmetry Principle (§1) — which was imported as a §1 structural principle "narrowly" per a separate hostile audit (commit `cf2f755`). The two R28 entries name distinct phenomena and live in different sections of the canon. The Speech-Act Announcement After Frame rule (this document's subject, in §5) and the Authorial Asymmetry Principle (in §1) share only the rule ID. This is a canon-numbering collision predating the migration to stable rule IDs; the migration should reserve a distinct stable ID for one of the two to eliminate ambiguity.

### 3. UD-detector implementation refinements (2026-04-19 / 2026-05-10)

The transition from a regex-based detector (`validate_rule_28_speech_act_after_frame.py`) to a UD-query detector (`validate_rule_28_ud.py`) tightened structural precision. The regex implementation matched same-line co-occurrence of a subordinating-conjunction-like surface token and a colon-terminated speech verb; the UD implementation verifies both structural conditions (matrix speech-VERB with `nsubj` AND advcl sibling/dependent) explicitly. The UD version eliminated false positives from constructions like *"as far as he said:"* where *as* is a degree adverb, not a subordinating conjunction.

### 4. Wave 6 filter refinements (2026-05-10)

Two filter refinements (result-direction filter, tightened `aux:pass` branch) were added during Wave 6 polish before the corpus sweep. Both were §7.3 trigger #6 changes (mechanical-signature changes under a settled rule). The refinements brought the detector's structural precision in line with the rule's grammatical intent; see Empirical evidence above for details.

### 5. T1.1 char-offset emission (2026-05-10)

The detector was migrated to emit `split_col` directly via `build_line_map_full`, matching the T1.1 char-offset pattern established for Rule 7's applier. This let the applier insert the break at the leftmost non-PUNCT speech-only token following the advcl-subtree, keeping any frame-closing comma on the BEFORE line. The applier (`apply_rule_28_ud.py`) skips entries with `split_col=None` (no clean split column — speech-tag structurally inside the advcl) or `split_col=0` (defensive).

### 6. Adoption sweep (2026-05-10)

27 STRONG-SPLIT-CANDIDATE applications across 7 books, 1 REVIEW-REQUIRED case correctly demoted. Adoption per §7.8 cleared the 80% threshold (96.4% clean). Status moved from Proposed to Active.

## Future work

### Cross-corpus consistency

The readers-gnt and readers-tanakh canons should formalize their own per-corpus instantiations of the J3 speech-act-after-frame sub-pattern, each calibrated to the target language's:

- Speech-verb closed list (Greek *εἶπεν*, *ἀπεκρίθη*, *ἐλάλησεν*, *ἔκραξεν*, *ἀπεφθέγξατο*; Hebrew *amar*-class, *daber*-class, *qara*-class, *anah*-class).
- Temporal/causal frame-mark inventory (Greek *ὅτε*, *ἐπεί*, *ὡς*, *μετὰ τὸ + inf.*; Hebrew *ka'asher*, *ki*, *ba'asher*, infinitive constructs with prepositional prefixes).
- Participial speech-continuation patterns (Greek *λέγων*, *εἰπών*; Hebrew *le'mor*).
- Result-subordinator inventory for the result-direction filter.

The cross-corpus pattern is stable: J3 sub-case, frame-mark closed list, speech-lemma closed list, participial-continuation exclusion, result-direction filter, true-absolute discriminator for participial frames.

### Canon rule-ID collision resolution

The two distinct phenomena both holding the ID "R28" (Speech-Act Announcement After Frame in §5; Authorial Asymmetry Principle in §1) are a legacy numbering collision. The migration to stable rule IDs should give one of them a fresh ID. Recommendation: rename the §1 Authorial Asymmetry Principle to a structural-principle ID (e.g., a §1.13 stable section ID without the R28 designation, since structural principles in §1 are not per-rule entries in §5).

### Long-frame edge cases

The current corpus sweep produced 27 mechanical applications and 1 REVIEW-REQUIRED case. A future audit could examine whether additional same-line frame+announcement cases exist that the detector misses because (a) the frame is multi-clause and the parser attaches differently, (b) the frame uses a less-common subordinator outside `FRAME_MARK_LEMMAS`, or (c) the speech verb is in a lemma outside `SPEECH_LEMMAS_R28` (e.g., *call*, *exhort*, *command*, *charge*, *demand*). The current closed list reflects the most common patterns; corpus-frequency-driven expansion is future work.

### REVIEW-REQUIRED case Alma 34:34

The single REVIEW-REQUIRED case from the adoption sweep is a genuine canon-gap residual: speech-tag *Ye cannot say* sits structurally inside the *when*-clause rather than after it. Whether this should be split (and if so, where) is editorial-judgment territory. A future per-instance review will resolve it and (if a generalizable pattern emerges) potentially add a sub-clause to R28.

### Petition-frame and J3 boundary

A subset of speech-verb advcl pairs reads ambiguously between the J3 speech-act announcement pattern (R28 territory) and the J5 substantive-adjunct-as-own-focus pattern (when the adverbial frame is content-substantial in its own right). The current closed-list filters keep this boundary clean for the common cases, but future audits may surface borderline shapes (e.g., long causal frames carrying their own communicative weight). The framework's §1.4 J5 and the detector's `FRAME_MARK_LEMMAS` closed list are the current adjudication; refinement is future work as residual cases surface.
